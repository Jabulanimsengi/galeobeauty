## Understanding How To Avoid Split Ends

A "split end" (scientifically termed *Trichoptilosis*) is exactly what it sounds like: the physical fracturing and splitting of the hair shaft at the tip. 

To understand why this happens, you have to look at the anatomy of your hair. Every hair strand is wrapped in overlapping microscopic "shingles" called the cuticle. At the very tip of your hair—which is the oldest part of the strand on your entire head—that cuticle has endured years of washing, brushing, heat styling, and environmental UV exposure. 

Eventually, those protective shingles wear completely away. Once the cuticle is gone, the inner core (the cortex) is exposed and completely unprotected. Being incredibly fragile, the cortex simply splits in half. Once a split begins at the tip, it does not stop; it acts exactly like a run in a pair of nylon stockings, traveling all the way up the hair shaft until the entire strand snaps off.

## Our Professional Advice

The most frustrating truth in the hair industry is that **you cannot repair a split end**. Once the hair has physically fractured, no product, serum, or oil on earth can magically fuse the dead tissue back together. You can only temporarily glue it flat until your next wash. Avoiding them entirely is the only real strategy.

### 1. The Preventative "Dusting"
If you refuse to cut your hair because you are trying to grow it out, you are ironically ensuring it will never get longer. If you do not cut the tiny splits off at the bottom, they will travel up the shaft and break the hair much higher up.
- **The Golden Rule:** You must book a [Micro-Trim or "Dusting"](/services/hair/cut-blow-long) every 8 to 10 weeks. Your stylist will literally just "dust" a quarter of an inch off the tips, removing the microscopic splits before they have the chance to travel up the shaft and cause massive breakage.

### 2. Turn Down the Heat
Extreme heat vaporizes the moisture inside the cortex of the oldest, most fragile part of your hair (the ends).
- **Thermal Lockdown:** Never run a flat iron over the tips of your hair multiple times. Pass the iron slowly, exactly *once*. If your tips are split, continuing to iron them will only fry them further. Ensure the ends are heavily coated in a premium thermal protectant before any heat touches them.

### 3. Lubricate the Tips Daily
Because the tips are the oldest part of the hair, they are historically the driest. The natural oil from your scalp cannot travel that far down the shaft.
- You must manually intervene. Every single morning, apply a tiny drop of lightweight, high-quality hair oil (like Argan or Marula oil) strictly to the bottom two inches of your hair. This creates artificial lubrication, allowing the tips to slide past your clothing and shoulders without snagging and fracturing.
