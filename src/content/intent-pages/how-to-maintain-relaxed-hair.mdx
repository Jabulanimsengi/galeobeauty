## Understanding How To Maintain Relaxed Hair

A hair relaxer is not simply a heavy conditioner; it is a profound chemical transformation. The alkaline chemicals in a relaxer violently force the hair cuticle open and purposefully shatter the disulfide bonds (the structural beams) that create the natural coil or curl pattern of the hair. 

Once those bonds are broken, the hair permanently loses its curl and hangs straight. Because you have chemically destroyed the internal scaffolding, relaxed hair is intrinsically weaker, vastly more porous, and incredibly vulnerable to snapping (breakage) compared to natural hair. 

To maintain relaxed hair, your single objective is to artificially replace the structural support you removed during the chemical process, while continuously injecting moisture to prevent the highly porous ends from drying out and shattering into split ends.

## Our Professional Advice

Maintaining relaxed hair requires a hyper-vigilant routine. You must strike a delicate, clinical balance between protein (to provide strength) and moisture (to provide elasticity).

### 1. The Strict Relaxer Schedule (Stretching)
The fastest way to destroy your hair is to overlap relaxer onto already-relaxed lengths. That is an absolute guarantee of a chemical haircut.
- **Root "Touch-Ups" Only:** You must only apply the chemical to new growth. 
- **Stretching Your Relaxer:** Do not relax your hair every 4 weeks. Attempt to "stretch" your relaxer touches to 8, 10, or even 12 weeks. During the stretch, rely heavily on deep conditioning and low-manipulation styles to manage the line of demarcation (where the fragile relaxed hair meets the strong new growth).

### 2. The Protein-Moisture Paramedic Routine
Because the internal bonds are broken, you must install temporary scaffolding.
- **Bi-Weekly Protein:** Use a light to medium protein reconstructor every 2 weeks. This fills the microscopic holes in the porous, relaxed hair shaft, giving it the structural rigidity required to not snap when brushed.
- **Weekly Intense Moisture:** Protein makes the hair strong, but it also makes it stiff. Stiff hair breaks. Always follow your protein treatments with an aggressive, heat-activated deeply hydrating mask to ensure the hair maintains flexibility.

### 3. Wrapping is Mandatory for Survival
Applying a flat iron to relaxed hair every single morning is a death sentence. The heat boils away what little moisture and elasticity remains.
- **The Wrap Method:** The golden rule of styling relaxed hair is wrapping it at night. Brush your hair entirely around your head in a circular motion, molding it flat against your scalp. Secure it tightly with a silk scarf. When you unwrap it in the morning, your body heat has smoothed it out perfectly—delivering a flat-ironed look with absolutely zero thermal damage.
