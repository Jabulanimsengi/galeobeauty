## Understanding How To Improve Hair Texture

A client's "hair texture" usually refers to exactly how the individual hair strands feel when rolled between the fingertips—whether they feel like fine silk, coarse wool, chaotic wire, or brittle straw. 

True biological texture is determined heavily by genetics and the physical shape of the follicle (which dictates if the hair grows out straight, wavy, or coiled). However, 90% of clients who complain about "bad hair texture" are actually complaining about **acquired surface damage**. 

When hair feels incredibly coarse, wiry, rough, or unmanageable, it means the hair's protective lipid layer has been permanently stripped away by external trauma. Without that lipid shield, the hair's cuticle lifts dramatically. Rather than running your fingers over a smooth glass cylinder, you are running your fingers over a microscopic pinecone.

## Our Professional Advice

You cannot change your genetic DNA, but you can dramatically transform acquired rough texture by implementing a heavy-duty "surfacing" regimen that artificially replaces the lost lipid layer.

### 1. The Hard Water Detox
If your texture suddenly changes and feels sticky, waxy, and unmanageable, your shower water is likely the culprit. 
- Hard water is loaded with calcium and magnesium. These minerals attach to the hair completely, creating an impenetrable mineral crust over the entire strand. This crust gives the hair a horrifyingly coarse texture.
- **The Solution:** Install a simple carbon shower filter, and immediately use a professional clarifying/chelating shampoo to chemically strip the mineral plaque off the hair.

### 2. Intense Cuticle Resurfacing
Because the hair cuticle is permanently blown open, standard hydrating products simply slide out. You have to fill in the missing structural gaps.
- Incorporate a heavy penetrating mask formulated specifically with Jojoba oil or Shea Butter twice a week. You must use heat (wrap your hair in a warm towel for 20 minutes) to force the dense butter molecules deep into the rough pinecone-like cuticles, temporarily smoothing the exterior down.

### 3. Smoothing Salon Treatments
If the texture of your hair makes it physically impossible to blow-dry yourself at home without spending an hour fighting with an iron, you require chemical assistance.
- **For Coarse/Frizzy Texture:** A [Keratin Smoothing Treatment](/prices/hair/keratin) acts exactly like microscopic spackle. The liquid keratin is baked into the rough gaps of the cuticle, entirely eliminating the coarse texture and replacing it with a heavily reinforced, completely smooth exterior shell.
- **For Dry/Brittle Texture:** A professional [Hair Botox](/prices/hair/botox) treatment acts as extreme anti-aging skin care for your hair, aggressively plumping up the shriveled, brittle strands with collagen and caviar oil to completely transform the tactile feel of the hair back to silk.
