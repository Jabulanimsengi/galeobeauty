## Understanding Sun Damage & Age Spots

To permanently eliminate dark "age spots" (solar lentigines), we must understand their chemical origin. Melanin is the microscopic pigment responsible for the color of your skin. It is heavily manufactured by cells called melanocytes at the very bottom of your epidermis. 

In a healthy state, melanin is distributed perfectly evenly. However, when your skin is repeatedly exposed to intense Ultraviolet (UV) radiation from the sun, your DNA believes it is under violent attack. In a desperate attempt to build a biological shield, the melanocytes go into extreme overdrive, pumping out massive, concentrated clumps of melanin exactly where the UV light struck hardest. 

These dense clumps of melanin rise to the surface and become permanently trapped in the dead layers of skin, forming thick, dark brown age spots. Because these spots are physically fused into the cellular matrix, no amount of casual scrubbing or weak drugstore vitamin C will remove them. The microscopic structure of the skin must be chemically dissolved.

## Our Professional Advice

Treating severe sun damage requires breaking the pigment apart mechanically, while simultaneously poisoning the enzyme that produces future pigment.

The dead, hyperpigmented cells must be forcibly removed.

### 2. Tyrosinase Inhibition (The Chemical Block)
You must stop the factory from producing more dark spots.
- **Tyrosinase** is the specific biological enzyme your body requires to physically manufacture melanin. A clinical brightening facial (like Dermalogica Pro Bright) forcefully infuses concentrated Vitamin C and Hexylresorcinol directly into the skin. These chemicals violently inhibit the Tyrosinase enzyme, ensuring your melanocytes physically cannot produce a new age spot, even if exposed to light.

### 3. Absolute SPF Compliance 
Pigment has an incredible biological memory.
- If you spend heavily on clinical peels to erase an age spot, and then walk into the sun for 10 minutes without SPF 50, the melanocytes will instantly remember the trauma and aggressively dump melanin back into the exact same spot. You must wear heavy sunscreen daily, or the treatments are a total waste of clinical resources.
