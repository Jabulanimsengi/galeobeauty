## Understanding How To Remove Blackheads

A blackhead (Open Comedone) is an impaction of hardened sebum and dead skin cells trapped inside a hair follicle. The "black" color is not dirt; it is the result of the oil oxidizing when it comes into contact with the air—much like an apple turns brown when cut.

The most common biological mistake clients make is attempting to "squeeze" blackheads at home or using adhesive "pore strips." These methods causes massive trauma to the follicular wall, often leading to permanent [Large Pores](/services/dermalogica) and broken capillaries. To remove a blackhead safely, you must first chemically soften the hardened plug before gently expressing it.

## Our Professional Advice

To achieve high-clarity skin, we recommend a clinical "Soft-and-Clear" protocol.

### 1. Desincrustation (The Softening)
Before any removal, our estheticians apply a specialized alkaline solution during a [ProSkin Facial](/services/dermalogica/pro-skin-60). This solution triggers a chemical process called **Saponification**, which literally turns the hardened, waxy oil inside your pores into a soft "liquid soap." This allows the blackhead to slide out of the pore without any damaging pressure.

### 2. Ultrasonic Extraction
Instead of using metal tools that can scar the skin, we utilize an **Ultrasonic Skin Scrubber**. This device vibrates at 28,000 cycles per second, creating microscopic "waves" that physically vibrate the dirt and oil out of the pores. It is the most non-invasive way to achieve a "deep-cleanse" without the redness associated with traditional extractions.

### 3. Pore-Refining Salicylic Acid
Once the pore is empty, it is biologically "open" and vulnerable. We apply a targeted 2% **Salicylic Acid** treatment. Because Salicylic acid is oil-soluble, it remains inside the pore, chemically preventing new oil from hardening and ensuring the blackheads do not return.
