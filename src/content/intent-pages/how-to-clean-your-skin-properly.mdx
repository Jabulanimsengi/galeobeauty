## Understanding How To Clean Your Skin Properly

Cleansing is, unequivocally, the most critical step in any skincare regimen, yet it is where the vast majority of clients induce massive self-harm. 

The goal of washing your face is simple: to remove pathogenic bacteria, environmental pollution from exhaust fumes, old makeup, and oxidizing sebum. 

However, many clients mentally associate a "clean" face with the sensation of the skin feeling completely tight, "squeaky clean," and devoid of all oil. This is a biological catastrophe. If your skin feels tight after you wash it, you have not just removed the dirt—you have chemically stripped away the skin’s **Acid Mantle**. 

The acid mantle is a microscopic, slightly acidic film of sweat and natural lipids that acts as an impenetrable armor against bacteria. When you use alkaline soaps or overly aggressive foaming cleansers, you annihilate this armor. Without it, moisture instantly evaporates out of your face (causing profound dryness and wrinkles), and acne-causing bacteria bypass the defenses with zero resistance.

## Our Professional Advice

To clean your skin properly, you must use clinical formulations that dissolve the dirt precisely without disturbing the protective lipid barrier. Here is the Galeo Beauty guide to washing your face like an esthetician.

### 1. Abolish Makeup Wipes
Makeup wipes are inherently disastrous for the skin. 
- They do not properly clean anything; they simply smear broken-down makeup, bacteria, and strong preservative chemicals violently across your entire face. The aggressive scrubbing motion micro-tears the fragile skin around your eyes, heavily accelerating premature wrinkles.

### 2. The Power of the Double Cleanse
You cannot wash away waterproof SPF and heavy liquid foundation with just water and a basic gel cleanser. Science dictates that "like dissolves like"—meaning only oil can dissolve heavy facial oil and silicone-based makeup.
- **Step One:** Massage a silky Cleansing Oil or Cleansing Balm directly onto *dry* skin with dirty makeup still on it. The oil magnetically attaches to the heavy foundation and SPF, instantly melting it into an emulsion. 
- **Step Two:** Add a tiny splash of water to turn the oil milky, rinse it, and then follow up with a gentle, non-stripping Gel Cleanser. The gel performs the actual deep pore clean now that the heavy makeup barrier is gone.

### 3. The 60-Second Rule
Do not just slap cleanser on your face for five seconds and immediately splash it off. 
- The active clinical ingredients in a high-quality cleanser require a minimum of 60 seconds of gentle, circular massaging contact with the skin to physically break down the sebum and penetrate the follicle. 

### 4. Water Temperature is Critical
Never wash your face in the shower under scalding hot water. 
- Extreme heat melts the healthy ceramides holding your skin cells together, causing severe redness and violently blasting open broken capillaries. You must exclusively wash your face using lukewarm or cool water at the sink.
