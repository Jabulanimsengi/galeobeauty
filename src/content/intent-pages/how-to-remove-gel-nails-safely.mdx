## Understanding How To Remove Gel Nails Safely

A gel manicure is designed to be highly impermeable. Once cured under a UV or LED lamp, the gel polish forms an incredibly tightly woven polymer matrix that acts like a layer of waterproof, flexible plastic bonded directly to your natural nail.

The most catastrophic mistake clients make with a gel manicure occurs exactly around week three. As the manicure grows out and a tiny corner begins to lift, the temptation to simply "peel" or "pick" the gel off like a sticker becomes overwhelming. 

**This is a critical error.** Gel polish does not just sit on top of the nail; it is molecularly fused to the keratin fibers of your nail plate's topmost layer. When you violently rip the gel polish off your nail, you are physically tearing the top protective layer of your biological nail completely off with it. This creates brutal "white spots" (areas where the nail has literally been ripped away), leaving your nails paper-thin, incredibly sensitive to hot water, and prone to severe snapping.

## Our Professional Advice

To remove gel polish without inflicting massive trauma to your nail bed, you must rely entirely on chemical dissolution rather than mechanical force. Follow the clinical removal protocol used safely in the Galeo Beauty [Nail Salon](/services/nails).

### 1. Breaking the Impermeable Seal 
Acetone physically cannot penetrate the glossy, protective top coat used to seal a gel manicure. 
- **The Protocol:** You must take a medium-grit nail file and firmly buff the shiny surface of every single nail until the gloss is completely removed and the nail looks entirely dull, dusty, and matte. You are essentially scratching open thousands of microscopic doorways so the acetone can enter the colored gel layers underneath.

### 2. The Foil Wrapping Matrix
Soaking your entire hand in a bowl of acetone is incredibly drying and damaging to your skin. The foil method creates a localized vapor chamber specifically over the nail.
- **The Protocol:** Heavily soak 10 small cotton balls in **100% Pure Acetone** (regular nail polish remover will not work). Place the heavily saturated cotton ball directly over the buffed nail. Take a pre-cut square of aluminum foil and tightly wrap it around the tip of your finger. The aluminum foil violently traps body heat and prevents the highly volatile acetone from evaporating into the air.

### 3. The 15-Minute Incubation
You must allow the chemistry to work.
- Let the foil wraps sit entirely undisturbed for a strict 15 minutes. During this time, the acetone is aggressively breaking the polymer bonds of the gel, turning the hard plastic back into a soft, crumbly powder.

### 4. Effortless Pushing (Never Scraping)
When you remove the foil wrap, the gel polish should be visibly "bubbling," flaking, and lifting aggressively off the nail plate.
- Gently push the crumbled gel off using a wooden orangewood stick. It should slide off with absolutely zero resistance. **If you have to scrape hard, stop.** The gel has not melted enough. Put the foil back on for 5 more minutes. If you aggressively scrape hard gel with a metal tool, you will physically gouge and ruin your natural nail bed.
