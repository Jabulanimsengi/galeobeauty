## Understanding How To Take Care Of Nails

Nail care is an exercise in **Keratin Preservation**. Your nail plate is composed of roughly 50 to 100 layers of flattened keratin cells. When these layers become dehydrated or mechanically traumatized, they separate (delaminate), leading to peeling, splitting, and breakage.

Most people treat their nails as "tools" rather than "appendages." Biologically, every time you use your nail to pry open a lid or scrape a surface, you are creating micro-fractures in the keratin matrix. To [Take Care of Your Nails](/services/nails), you must protect the structural integrity of these layers and maintain their internal lipid balance.

## Our Professional Advice

A clinical nail care routine focuses on flexibility, hydration, and mechanical protection.

### 1. The Jojoba Protocol
Jojoba oil is the only oil with a molecular structure small enough to actually penetrate the nail plate. Standard mineral oils just sit on top. By applying Jojoba oil to your nails twice daily, you restore the internal "flex" of the keratin. This ensures that when you bump your nail, it **bends and bounces** back instead of snapping off.

### 2. Sealing the Free Edge
The "Free Edge" (the white tip) of your nail is where most damage begins. When you file your nails roughly or leave the edge unfinished, water and chemicals can enter *between* the keratin layers. You must use a high-quality glass file to "seal" the edge, creating a smooth, impermeable barrier that prevents delamination.

### 3. Avoid Prolonged Water Submersion
Water is the ultimate solvent for keratin. When your nails are submerged in water for more than 10 minutes, they absorb massive amounts of moisture, swell, and then rapidly contract as they dry. This "expansion/contraction" cycle violently breaks the internal bonds of the nail. Always wear gloves when cleaning or doing dishes to maintain your [Manicure](/services/nails) and natural nail health.
