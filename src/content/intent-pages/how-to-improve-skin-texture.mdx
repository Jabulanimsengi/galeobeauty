## Understanding How To Improve Skin Texture

When a client complains about "bad skin texture," they are usually describing a surface that feels like fine sandpaper, appears dimpled (like an orange peel), or is riddled with tiny, stubborn, skin-colored bumps holding absolutely no pus.

Skin texture is rarely a singular issue; it is a physical manifestation of microscopic dermal chaos. The three primary culprits driving uneven texture are:
1. **Severe Dehydration (Not Dryness):** Your skin lacks water. The cells literally shrivel up like raisins. When microscopic cells are shriveled, the surface of your skin feels incredibly rough and crinkled instead of smooth and bouncy.
2. **Micro-Comedones (Congestion):** Your pores are filling up with hard, oxidized sebum (oil) and dead skin, but the blockage hasn't inflamed into an actual acne pimple. These create thousands of tiny, hard bumps under the surface (often on the forehead or jawline).
3. **Collagen Degradation (Orange Peel Skin):** As the collagen around your pores weakens due to sun damage and age, the pores violently stretch out, creating a permanent, pitted "orange peel" texture.

## Our Professional Advice

You cannot "scrub" your way to smooth skin texture. Attempting to buff off tiny bumps with a harsh physical apricot scrub will only create microscopic lacerations, inducing inflammation and making the texture substantially worse. You must chemically remodel the surface.

### 1. The AHA Chemical Remodeling
To get rid of the rough, sandpaper-like dead skin buildup, you must dissolve the intercellular glue.
- **The Routine:** Introduce a **Glycolic Acid or Lactic Acid** leave-on exfoliant 3 times a week. These Alpha Hydroxy Acids (AHAs) cleanly sever the bonds holding the dead skin on the surface, revealing the microscopically smooth, fresh skin underneath entirely without scrubbing.

### 2. Deep Pore Desincrustation (BHA)
If your texture is primarily caused by thousands of tiny, hard, skin-colored bumps (congestion), AHA will not reach deep enough.
- **The Routine:** You need a **Salicylic Acid (BHA)** toner. Because BHA is oil-soluble, it plunges deep into the congested pore and politically dissolves the hardened wax and oil from the inside out, effectively melting the tiny bumps away over four to six weeks.

### 3. Flooding the Dermis (Hydration Plumping)
You must reverse the "raisin" effect.
- **The Routine:** Immediately after washing your face while it is still damp, flood your skin with a pure Hyaluronic Acid serum. Seal it immediately with a ceramide-rich moisturizer. The cells will aggressively suck the water in, instantly plumping up the shriveled skin and smoothing out the crinkled surface texture.

### 4. Advanced Clinical Microneedling
If your bad texture is caused by pitted acne scars (indented craters) or severely enlarged "orange peel" pores, no topical cream will fix it.
- **The Solution:** You must undergo [Clinical Microneedling](/prices/dermalogica). By deliberately creating microscopic puncture wounds in the dermis, the body is forced to flood the scarred, pitted areas with massive amounts of brand new collagen, physically pushing the indented scars up to be perfectly level with the rest of your skin.
