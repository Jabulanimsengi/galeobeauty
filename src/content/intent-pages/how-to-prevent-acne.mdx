## Understanding How To Prevent Acne

Treating an existing pimple is a reactive measure; it means the inflammatory cascade has already breached the pore and caused damage. True clear skin is achieved proactively—by creating an external biological environment where the acne bacteria (*C. acnes*) finds it mathematically impossible to survive and multiply.

Preventing acne requires a clinical focus on two objectives: regulating the shedding of dead skin cells so they do not form a "plug" over the pore, and meticulously controlling the bacterial load on your face without destroying your skin's protective acid mantle.

If you are constantly battling new breakouts every single week, your preventative habits are likely failing at a microscopic level.

## Our Professional Advice

At Galeo Beauty, our dermal therapists emphasize that acne prevention is a 360-degree lifestyle protocol. If you use a $100 clinical serum but sleep on a bacteria-infested pillowcase, you will continue to break out.

### 1. The Non-Comedogenic Mandate
Take every single liquid foundation, sunscreen, and moisturizer you own and check the ingredient list.
- If a product contains heavy, pore-blocking ingredients (like Coconut Oil, Lanolin, or Isopropyl Myristate), it is physically packing your pores with sludge every morning. You must exclusively purchase products explicitly labeled **"Non-Comedogenic"** (meaning they have been clinically tested not to block pores).

### 2. Pillowcases and Phones
You are violently rubbing your face against two incredibly dirty surfaces for hours every single day.
- **Your Phone:** Your cell phone screen harbors more bacteria than a public restroom. When you press it against your cheek to make a phone call, you are stamping that bacteria directly into your pores. Use speakerphone, or sanitize your screen daily.
- **Your Pillowcase:** When you sleep, you sweat oil and shed dead skin cells directly into your cotton pillowcase. If you do not change your pillowcase every 3 days, you are grinding your face into a Petri dish of old bacteria for 8 hours a night.

### 3. The Preventative Exfoliation Protocol
You cannot rely on physical scrubs (like walnut shells) to clear pores; they just scratch the surface.
- To prevent acne, you must use a chemical exfoliant twice a week. A leave-on **AHA (Lactic or Glycolic Acid)** serum will gently dissolve the surface glue holding dead skin cells together, ensuring they harmlessly fall away rather than getting jammed down into your hair follicles to start a massive breakout.

### 4. Hydration is Mandatory
The most common mistake acne-prone clients make is attempting to "dry out" their oily skin by using harsh alcohol toners and skipping moisturizer entirely.
- If you strip all the moisture from your face, your skin panics and violently over-produces *more* oil to compensate for the extreme dryness, creating a massive acne flare-up. You perfectly prevent this by using a lightweight, oil-free gel moisturizer every single night to trick the skin into believing it has enough hydration.
