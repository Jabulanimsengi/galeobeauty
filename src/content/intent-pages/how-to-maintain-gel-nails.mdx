## Understanding How To Maintain Gel Nails

A professional Gel Manicure is chemically engineered to be virtually indestructible under normal human conditions. The heavy photo-initiator reaction triggered by the UV lamp creates a tightly woven polymer matrix that acts like flexible plastic armor bonded directly to the keratin proteins of your natural nail.

However, "indestructible" does not mean invincible. When gel nails prematurely peel, violently chip, or lift in massive sheets within the first week, it is typically due to a failure in the client's home-care maintenance. 

The primary enemy of a gel manicure is fluid dynamics—specifically, hot water and harsh solvents. Because your natural nail plate is highly porous (like a sponge), it expands when wet and contracts when dry. The gel polish layered on top is essentially waterproof plastic. If your natural nail underneath is constantly expanding and contracting, the rigid plastic gel on top cannot keep up with the movement and eventually violently detaches from the nail bed.

## Our Professional Advice

To force your [Gel Manicure](/prices/nails) to last a flawless 3 to 4 weeks, you must ruthlessly protect the chemical bond from environmental degradation. Here is the strict Galeo Beauty protocol.

### 1. Gloves are Mandatory, Not Optional
You cannot use your bare hands to wash dishes or clean the bathroom. This is the fastest way to destroy a gel manicure.
- **The Rule:** Hot water aggressively softens the gel polish, while intense dish soaps and chemical household cleaners bleach the color and shatter the polymer bonds holding the gel to your nail. You absolutely must wear heavy rubber gloves anytime your hands will be submerged in water for longer than 3 minutes, or when handling any cleaning chemicals.

### 2. Stop Using Your Nails as Tools
Gel polish makes your nails feel incredibly strong, leading to dangerous over-confidence.
- Your nails are jewels, not tools. You cannot use your freshly manicured nail to aggressively pry open a soda can, aggressively scratch off a sticker, or unscrew a tight lid. The sheer mechanical force placed directly on the free edge of the nail will instantly fracture the gel seal, causing an immediate, massive chip. Use a butter knife or your knuckles.

### 3. The Cuticle Oil Command
Hydration is the absolute secret to a long-lasting manicure. 
- Your natural nail requires intense lipids to remain flexible. If your natural nail becomes dry and brittle under the gel plate, it will snap, taking the gel polish with it. 
- **The Protocol:** You must apply a tiny drop of pure Jojoba-based cuticle oil to the base of every nail every single night. Jojoba oil is molecularly small enough to penetrate the microscopic gaps around the gel, keeping the natural nail completely nourished and preventing the horrifying "white lifting" along the cuticle line.

### 4. Never, Ever Peel
When a tiny chip finally occurs, your brain will violently urge you to peel the rest of the gel off.
- **The Warning:** Gel polish is aggressively bonded to the top layer of your natural nail plate. If you forcefully rip the gel off like a sticker, you are physically tearing off the entire protective top layer of your biological nail. This leaves your natural nails paper-thin, severely damaged, and highly sensitive to hot and cold temperatures. Keep a nail file handy to smooth out chips until you can visit a professional.
