## Understanding How To Maintain Hair Extensions

Hair extensions—whether they are tape-ins, micro-links, wefts, or keratin bonds—are a significant financial investment designed to instantly provide transformative volume and length.

However, the golden rule of extensions is understanding that no matter how premium the human hair is, **it is no longer attached to a living scalp.**

Your natural hair thrives because your scalp constantly secretes sebum (oil) that travels down the shaft, keeping the cuticle naturally moisturized and protected against the elements. Extensions receive absolutely none of this biological hydration. Consequently, if you do not manually and aggressively hydrate them, they will dry out, mat, tangle, and become completely unmanageable within weeks.

## Our Professional Advice

Maintaining extensions requires a specialized, gentle routine that prioritizes extreme hydration while protecting the delicate anchor points near your roots.

### 1. Strategic Washing
You should significantly reduce how frequently you wash your hair. Washing 1-2 times a week is ideal.
- **Sulfate-Free Only:** Sulfates are harsh detergents that strip away moisture. Because your extensions do not produce their own oil, sulfates will destroy them. Use only professional sulfate-free shampoos.
- **Never wash upside down:** Flipping your head over the sink or bath reverses the natural direction of the extensions, putting massive stress on the bonds or tapes and guaranteeing severe matting. Wash upright in the shower, letting the water flow down the lengths.
- **Conditioning:** Apply heavy conditioner *only* to the mid-lengths and ends. If heavy oils or conditioners reach your roots, they will cause the extension tapes or bonds to slip and slide right out of your hair.

### 2. Masterful Brushing
Brushing extensions requires time and patience.
- **Support the Roots:** Whenever you brush, hold the hair tightly at the roots with your free hand. This absorbs the pulling tension so you do not rip the extensions out of your scalp.
- **Bottom-Up:** Start brushing at the very bottom tips and slowly work your way up. Use an extension-specific loop brush or a wet-detangler that does not snag on the attachment points.

### 3. Protective Sleeping
The worst damage to extensions happens while you sleep.
- Going to bed with wet extensions is an absolute disaster; they will mat into dreadlocks by morning. Ensure the roots are 100% bone dry before sleeping.
- Sleep on a silk or satin pillowcase to reduce friction, and always tie your hair in a loose, low braid. This prevents the long lengths from wrapping around themselves during the night.
