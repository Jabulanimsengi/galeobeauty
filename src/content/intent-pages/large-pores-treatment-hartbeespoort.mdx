## Why pores can suddenly start looking worse

Visible pores are often not just about pore size itself. They usually become more obvious when the skin is congested, oily, rough in texture, or starting to lose some of its firmness.

That is why one person needs deep cleansing first, while another needs texture-focused correction.

## When clearing work is the better starting point

If your pores are filling quickly with oil and blackheads, the more useful first step is often a pore-clearing treatment rather than jumping straight into a stronger resurfacing option. In those cases, [Hydraderm](/services/dermalogica/hydraderm) or another deep-cleansing facial may make the most visible difference early on.

## When texture or firmness is the bigger issue

If the pores are not only clogged but the skin also feels uneven or makeup sinks into the texture, [Pro Microneedling](/services/dermalogica/pro-microneedling) may be more relevant later. That is usually the option people consider when the concern is moving from congestion into texture and firmness support.

## Where a peel fits in

[Pro Power Peel](/services/dermalogica/pro-power-peel) can be useful when dull surface build-up, roughness and uneven texture are adding to the appearance of enlarged pores. It is often less about "shrinking" the pore and more about making the surrounding skin look smoother and more refined.

## How to choose the better first booking

- Start with deep cleansing if the main issue is blocked pores and blackheads.
- Ask about a peel if the skin looks rough, dull or built up.
- Ask about microneedling if the concern is more about texture and firmness than congestion alone.

The best pore-focused treatment is usually the one that tackles what is making the pores look more obvious in the first place.
