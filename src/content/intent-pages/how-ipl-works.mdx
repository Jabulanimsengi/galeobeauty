## Understanding How IPL Works

Intense Pulsed Light (IPL) is frequently confused with traditional laser technology, but their underlying physics are entirely different. 

A traditional laser emits a single, highly concentrated, monochromatic wavelength of light that travels in a perfectly straight, focused beam (like a sniper rifle). **IPL technology**, however, uses a broad-spectrum flashlamp. It emits a massive, scattered spectrum of light encompassing multiple wavelengths at once (between 500 and 1,200 nanometers) in a single chaotic flash, acting more like an incredibly powerful shotgun or high-intensity camera flash.

The magic of IPL lies in **Selective Photothermolysis**. The scattered light energy is violently absorbed by specific highly-pigmented targets in the skin—specifically, dark melanin (in hair follicles or brown sunspots) and hemoglobin (red blood in broken capillaries). When these dark targets absorb the intense light energy, it instantly converts into catastrophic heat. This heat completely incinerates the target (destroying the hair bulb or coagulating the broken vein) while leaving the surrounding, lighter-colored healthy skin entirely untouched.

## Our Professional Advice

Because IPL relies on color contrast, it is a highly selective tool. It requires specific biological parameters to work effectively. Here is how Galeo Beauty safely utilizes IPL physics.

### 1. The Necessary Contrast (Hair vs. Skin)
The IPL machine is essentially "blind"; it only sees color. 
- The light violently attacks whatever is darkest. This is why IPL is incredibly effective on clients with light skin and extremely dark, thick hair. The light completely ignores the light skin and instantly plunges down into the dark root of the hair.
- If a client has very dark skin, or very light blonde/white hair, IPL can be highly dangerous or entirely ineffective. If the skin is darker than the hair, the machine will accidentally attack the skin, causing severe dermal burns. A thorough [Professional Consultation](/services/dermalogica) is mandatory before any IPL session.

### 2. Hair Removal vs. Photorejuvenation
Because IPL uses *multiple* wavelengths simultaneously, we can use different glass filters to completely change what the machine attacks.
- **For Hair Removal:** We use a filter that specifically blocks out all colors except the exact wavelength that melanin (hair) absorbs, permanently destroying the blood supply to the hair follicle.
- **For Skin Rejuvenation:** We change the glass filter to target hemoglobin (the red in broken blood vessels) and excess melanin (brown age spots). The IPL flash violently shatters the brown pigment into microscopic dust that flakes off days later, while instantly shrink-wrapping and closing broken red capillaries.

### 3. The Absolute Law of SPF
IPL makes your skin photo-sensitized. 
- The treatment deliberately causes a micro-thermal injury to your skin's pigment cells. If you undergo an IPL session and immediately walk out into the direct sun without a heavy physical **SPF 50 sunscreen**, you will incur massive, permanent hyperpigmentation damage that is far worse than the original spots you were trying to treat.
