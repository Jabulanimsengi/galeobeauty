## Understanding How To Fix Over Processed Hair

"Over-processed" is the salon term for hair that has been subjected to so many overlapping chemical services (bleaching, relaxing, perming, or high-heat styling) that the fundamental internal architecture of the hair strand has completely collapsed.

Healthy hair has a smooth, tightly closed exterior cuticle and an interior cortex held together by strong disulfide bonds. When you over-process hair—for example, putting bleach on top of already-bleached hair—the alkaline chemicals literally dissolve the cuticle and shatter those internal bonds permanently. 

The result is extremely compromised "cotton candy" hair. When wet, it feels mushy, slimy, and stretches like an elastic band until it disintegrates. When dry, it feels violently brittle, stiff, and snaps off when you attempt to push a brush through it. Over-processed hair is incapable of holding onto moisture or color pigment because there is no cuticle left to lock it inside.

## Our Professional Advice

The brutal truth is that you cannot resurrect dead, over-processed tissue. You cannot "heal" it. Your only option is to artificially reinforce it like a cast on a broken bone to prevent the hair from entirely snapping off while it slowly grows out.

### 1. Immediately Stop All Chemical Services
This is non-negotiable. If you try to correct brassy, over-processed blonde hair by putting more bleach or high-lift developer on it, the hair will simply fall off entirely, resulting in a chemical haircut. 
- You must cease all coloring, highlighting, perm, and relaxing services immediately. 
- If you must color your hair, strictly use a deposit-only (zero developer) **Color Gloss** to add pigment without opening the cuticle.

### 2. Clinical Bond Builders
Because the internal scaffolding is destroyed, regular hydrating conditioners are useless.
- You must use hardcore structural repair technology like **Olaplex No. 0 & No. 3** or a **K18 Molecular Repair Mask**. These products penetrate the mushy cortex and artificially reconnect the broken polypeptide chains. Use them religiously twice a week.

### 3. The Professional "Chop"
If the bottom four inches of your hair are transparent, gooey when wet, and snapping off on your shoulders, you must cut them off. 
- Leaving destroyed ends attached means the split will continue breaking upwards, eventually destroying the healthy hair near the root. Book a transformative [Haircut and Style](/prices/hair/cut-blow-medium) to instantly make the hair look drastically thicker and healthier.

### 4. Banish the Hot Tools
You absolutely cannot use a 400-degree flat iron on over-processed hair. It will instantly fry the fragile remnants of the strand. 
- Adopt heatless styling methods, completely drench the hair in protective leave-in conditioners every single morning, and limit blow-drying to the "cool" or "medium" setting only.
