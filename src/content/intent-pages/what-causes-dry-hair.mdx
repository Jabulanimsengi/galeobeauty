## Understanding What Causes Dry Hair

Chronic dry hair feels coarse, looks incredibly dull, refuses to hold a style, and is horrifyingly prone to mid-shaft breakage. Despite how hopeless it feels to touch bone-dry hair, the underlying causes are usually identifiable and almost always fixable.

Dry hair occurs because of two primary mechanisms:
1. **The Root Level Problem:** Your scalp is not producing enough natural sebum (oil) to adequately travel down the hair shaft and moisturize the lengths.
2. **The Cuticle Level Problem:** Your scalp *is* producing enough oil, but the cuticle armor of your hair is so damaged, highly porous, and stripped that it simply cannot hold onto the moisture for more than a few hours before it evaporates into the atmosphere.

As hair specialists, we categorize these causes into intrinsic (genetic/internal) factors and extrinsic (environmental/mechanical) factors.

## Our Professional Advice

Before you can permanently treat your dry hair with intensive conditioning, you must figure out exactly what is causing the moisture to leak out in the first place.

### 1. Genetic and Structural Causes
If you have tight, coily, or curly hair, your hair is intrinsically prone to profound dryness.
- The sebaceous glands on your scalp release oil designed to travel down the hair shaft via gravity. For clients with bone-straight hair, this is a smooth ride. For clients with curls, the oil struggles to navigate the twists, turns, and coils, leaving the mid-lengths and ends completely unguarded. 
- *The Solution:* You must intervene by manually applying lightweight carrier oils to the ends and heavily utilizing leave-in hydration complexes every single day.

### 2. Environmental Trauma
You might be systematically destroying your hair's hydration without knowing it.
- **Sun Overexposure:** Just as the sun bakes your skin, intense UV rays break down the lipids inside the hair shaft, vaporizing moisture.
- **Hard Water:** If your shower utilizes "hard water," the heavy minerals (like calcium and magnesium) create an impenetrable film over the hair. This film blocks your expensive conditioners from actually penetrating the cuticle. Over time, the hair starves beneath the crust. Consider installing a basic shower-head filter!

### 3. Thermal and Chemical Abuse
This is the most common reason clients end up in our salon chairs.
- Applying a 400-degree flat iron to your hair physically boils the water inside the cortex until it turns to steam and violently blasts out of the cuticle.
- Heavy oxidative processing, like repeated bleaching sessions, irreparably destroys the cuticle flaps. If the cuticle cannot close, no amount of deep-conditioning masks will stay inside the hair. If you suffer from massive chemical damage, stop booking color sessions and immediately consult a professional regarding intensive [bond-building restorations](/prices/hair).
