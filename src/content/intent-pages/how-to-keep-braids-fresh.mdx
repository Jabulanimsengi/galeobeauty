## Understanding How To Keep Braids Fresh

Protective styles—such as box braids, knotless braids, or twists—are absolutely incredible for retaining length because they tuck the fragile ends of your natural hair safely away from environmental friction.

However, a massive misconception is that a protective style means you have a "vacation" from hair care. If you completely neglect the braids for six weeks, one of two horrific realities will occur: either your scalp will become suffocated with extreme bacterial buildup and dandruff, or your entire natural hair strand will severely dehydrate, locking into macroscopic, un-brushable dreadlocks when you attempt to remove the style.

A protective style is only "protective" if you are actively maintaining the internal moisture of the hair trapped inside, and meticulously ensuring the scalp remains sterile and oxygenated.

## Our Professional Advice

Maintaining fresh-looking braids that don't destroy your underlying hair requires a strict weekly regimen focused almost exclusively on the root and the scalp.

### 1. The Witch Hazel Scalp Cleanse
You cannot drench your braids in a heavy shower wash every few days; they will become massively heavy, put extreme traction on your roots, and lock frizz permanently into the style.
- You must clean only the scalp. Once a week, dip a cotton pad into a mixture of Witch Hazel and an anti-bacterial essential oil (like Tea Tree oil). Systematically wipe down every single exposed "part" on your scalp grid. This removes excess sebum, dead skin cells, and eliminates the itch-inducing bacteria without fuzzing up the braid.

### 2. Liquid Hydration on the Lengths
Remember, your natural hair is physically trapped inside dry synthetic fibers. The synthetic hair sucks the moisture right out of your natural hair.
- You cannot use heavy creams on braids (they will turn into a white, gunky, crusty mess). 
- Every other day, completely mist your braids from root to tip with a **water-based liquid leave-in spray** (look for Aloe Vera and Glycerin bases). 

### 3. The Oil Seal
Once the liquid hydration is sprayed on, seal the exposed scalp using a light sealing oil so it doesn't evaporate. 
- Apply a lightweight oil (like Sweet Almond or Jojoba oil) strictly to the scalp parts using an applicator bottle to prevent dryness and flaking.

### 4. Nighttime Defense Armor
Friction is what turns beautiful, sleek braids into a fuzzy, messy disaster within two weeks.
- You absolutely must sleep in a silk or satin bonnet, or sleep on a high-quality silk pillowcase. If you have long braids, you must tie them up into a loose bun on the top of your head before putting the bonnet on so you do not roll over them and physically yank your own roots out while you sleep.
