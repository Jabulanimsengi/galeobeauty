## Understanding How To Fix Broken Nails

A broken nail is rarely just an annoyance; if the nail violently tears low enough into the pink "nail bed" (the living tissue highly saturated with nerves and blood vessels), it becomes a highly painful, open mechanical wound. 

Biologically, the visible nail plate is composed entirely of dead, hardened keratin cells. Because these cells are already dead, the nail physically possesses absolutely no ability to "heal" itself. You cannot apply a magic serum to fuse a snapped nail backward. Once the keratin bonds mathematically break, that architectural breach is permanent. 

The strategy for fixing a massive tear or severe break relies entirely on creating an artificial splint. You must synthesize a rigid chemical "bridge" over the violent tear to lock the moving pieces completely together. This stops the jagged crack from snagging on clothing and ripping deeper into the painful, live tissue until the break naturally grows out past the free edge.

## Our Professional Advice

If you have a disastrous tear low down on the nail bed, immediately stop panicking and do not attempt to rip the dangling piece completely off. Here is the professional architectural repair protocol.

### 1. The Teabag Splint Method (At-Home Rescue)
If you cannot safely make it to a salon, you can manufacture an emergency fiberglass wrap using household items to save the torn nail.
- **The Materials:** You need standard nail glue (cyanoacrylate) and a tiny, clean square cut from an empty paper tea bag. 
- **The Protocol:** Gently file the surface over the tear so it is slightly rough. Apply a generous drop of nail glue directly over the violent crack. Using tweezers, immediately place the tiny square of the teabag paper directly into the wet glue over the tear. The paper acts precisely like a fiberglass splint, absorbing the glue and hardening into an incredibly strong, rigid cast. Once it is completely dry, buff it smoothly and cover it with a protective clear coat.

### 2. Professional Silk Wrap or Builder Gel
If the tear is extremely painful, deep, and bleeding, the absolute safest intervention is a [Professional Nail Repair](/services/nails) at the salon.
- **The Professional Solution:** A master technician will properly sanitize the microscopic wound to prevent Staph infection. They will then apply professional-grade silk or fiberglass wraps, heavily soaking them in hard builder gel. The powerful UV light will cure the gel into rock-hard plastic armor over the catastrophic break, totally immobilizing the tear and allowing it to safely grow out pain-free over the next three weeks.

### 3. Never "File Out" a Deep Rip
If the tear happens on the side of the nail (the sidewall), do not aggressively use an emery board to file the edge all the way down to a sharp slant just to "get rid of" the piece.
- By filing deeply into the side of the nail plate, you violently remove the structural support of the nail. As it grows, it will inevitably snag and rip entirely across the middle of the nail bed. A tear must be splinted, not filed away.

### 4. Preventative Oil Defense
Nails only snap violently when they are brutally dehydrated and brittle.
- To prevent this from ever happening again, heavily soak your nails in pure Jojoba oil. A perfectly hydrated nail plate will bend deeply upon heavy impact and immediately bounce back, whereas a dry nail plate will instantly shatter.
