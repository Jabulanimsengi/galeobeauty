## Understanding How To Maintain IPL Results

Intense Pulsed Light (IPL) treatments are highly investments that effectively erase decades of chronological damage off the face. They beautifully coagulate broken capillaries (redness) and completely shatter deep pockets of melanin (brown age spots). 

However, IPL does not fundamentally alter your DNA. The treatment erases *past* damage, but it does absolutely nothing to prevent *future* damage from accumulating. If a client undergoes six expensive IPL sessions, archives absolutely flawless, "glass skin," and then immediately resumes laying out in the sun or utilizing terrible skincare habits, the exact same hyperpigmentation and redness will violently return within six months. 

Maintaining the profound results of an IPL series requires a total behavioral shift. You must aggressively shield the new, vulnerable skin from oxidative stress and UV radiation, while utilizing potent at-home chemical maintenance to keep the cellular turnover rate unnaturally fast.

## Our Professional Advice

At Galeo Beauty, we insist clients adopt a "protect and defend" homecare regimen following their final [IPL Photofacial](/services/dermalogica). Here is the master protocol to lock in your results for years.

### 1. The Non-Negotiable SPF Armor
UV radiation is the sole reason you required IPL to begin with.
- The new, unblemished skin revealed after IPL is incredibly fragile. UV rays will immediately penetrate this pristine skin and violently reactivate the melanocyte factories, producing new, deep brown spots. 
- **The Rule:** You absolutely must wear a highly reflective, broad-spectrum **Physical Sunscreen (Zinc Oxide or Titanium Dioxide)**. Physical sunscreens act as literal mirrors, bouncing the UV rays completely off your face before they can even touch the live skin cells. Reapply every 2 hours if outdoors.

### 2. Tyrosinase Blockers (Vitamin C)
Even with heavy sunscreen, you must chemically suppress the pigment factory from turning back on.
- **The Routine:** Every single morning, apply a heavily concentrated 15% L-Ascorbic Acid (Vitamin C) serum beneath your sunscreen. Vitamin C acts as a "Tyrosinase Inhibitor"—it literally severs the biological communication line that tells your skin to produce brown melanin. It acts as an internal, chemical shield against new sun damage.

### 3. Nighttime Cellular Turnover (Retinol)
To keep the skin looking intensely tight, glassy, and continuously even-toned, you cannot allow dead skin to build back up.
- **The Routine:** Introduce a high-quality clinical Retinoid (Vitamin A) into your nightly routine. Retinol forcefully accelerates cellular turnover, aggressively pushing out faint shadows of pigment before they can become massive, stubborn brown spots, while constantly stimulating fresh collagen production to maintain the "tightness" achieved during the IPL sessions.

### 4. Prevent Vascular Trauma
If your IPL targeted redness and broken capillaries (like Rosacea or Spider Veins), your blood vessels are simply fragile by genetics.
- Stop subjecting your face to extreme vascular stress. Stop violently scrubbing your cheeks with rough cloths. Stop taking boiling hot showers where the heat beats directly onto your face. Extreme mechanical scrubbing and thermal heat will violently burst brand new capillaries directly under the skin, requiring another round of IPL to collapse them again.
