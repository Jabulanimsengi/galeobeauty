## Understanding How To Maintain Beauty Results

Investing in professional beauty treatments (like Peels, Microblading, or Keratin) is only the beginning of the biological journey. Biologically, your body is in a constant state of "Self-Correction." It is actively working to shed the pigment we implanted, relax the curl we set, and exfoliate the skin we resurfaced.

To maintain your [Beauty Results](/prices), you must interrupt the body's natural return to its baseline state. This requires a dedicated homecare protocol that mimics the chemistry of the salon treatment. If you use generic, supermarket-grade products after a clinical treatment, you will physically wash your investment down the drain in less than a week.

## Our Professional Advice

To maximize the longevity of your professional treatments, you must follow the "Three Pillars of Preservation."

### 1. pH Synchronization
Professional treatments often alter the pH level of your skin or hair to achieve a result. To maintain that result, your homecare must be pH-synchronized. For example, after a [Keratin Treatment](/prices/hair/smoothing), you must use sulphate-free, acidic shampoos to keep the hair cuticle "clamped" shut, locking the keratin inside the shaft horizontally.

### 2. Lipid Replenishment
Clinical procedures (especially peels and laser) temporarily deplete the skin's natural lipid barrier. To prevent "rebound inflammation," you must flood the skin with **Ceramides and Essential Fatty Acids**. This keeps the skin "soft" and flexible, preventing it from cracking and shedding prematurely.

### 3. Progressive Frequency
Beauty results are cumulative. You cannot "fix" a concern in one session and expect it to stay fixed forever. Maintenance requires a progressive schedule. We recommend returning to the salon *before* the results have completely faded—this allows us to "stack" the clinical effects, leading to a permanent improvement in your biological baseline over 12 months.
