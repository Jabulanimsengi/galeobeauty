## Understanding How To Maintain Smooth Skin

Smooth skin (high-refinement texture) is a result of a perfectly organized **Stratum Corneum**. When your skin is "rough," it is because dead skin cells are desquamating (shedding) unevenly, creating a microscopic "mountain range" on your face that scatters light and feels like sandpaper.

Biologically, maintaining smoothness requires **Uniform Exfoliation** and **Dermal Plumping**. You must remove the "mountains" of dead skin while simultaneously filling the cells beneath with enough moisture so they push upward against the surface, creating a tight, glassy appearance.

## Our Professional Advice

Achieving and maintaining a "glass skin" texture requires a delicate balance of chemical resurfacing and deep hydration.

### 1. Liquid Exfoliation (AHAs)
Stop using physical facial scrubs. They create microscopic "tears" in the skin that actually lead to more roughness. Instead, use a daily **Lactic Acid or Mandelic Acid toner**. These Alpha Hydroxy Acids (AHAs) dissolve the microscopic "glue" holding dead cells to the surface, allowing them to shed in a perfectly uniform sheet, revealing the smooth skin beneath.

### 2. Nightly Retinoid Stimulation
To maintain smoothness long-term, you must communicate with your basal cells. **Retinol** signals your skin to produce new cells at a much faster rate. This "push from below" ensures that your skin surface is always composed of fresh, hydrated, and perfectly flat cells.

### 3. High-Performance Hydration
Smoothness is also a function of volume. When skin is dehydrated, it shrivels, making pores and fine lines appear magnified. We recommend regular [ProSkin Facials](/services/dermalogica/pro-skin-60) utilizing ultrasonic infusion to drive high-molecular-weight Hyaluronic Acid deep into the dermis, creating a "plumped" surface that is naturally smooth to the touch.
