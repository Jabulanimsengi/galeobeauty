## Understanding How To Care For Acrylic Nails

Acrylic nails provide profound, structural rigidity. For clients with incredibly brittle, short natural nails, the heavy monomer/polymer matrix offers the ability to instantly manufacture massive, beautiful length that will not bend or break during daily activities.

However, the immense strength of an acrylic enhancement is also its greatest physiological risk. The acrylic is structurally bonded directly to the top layer of your natural nail plate. Because the acrylic itself is practically indestructible, any violent mechanical force applied to the tip of the fake nail will not snap the acrylic—it will transfer that terrifying force straight down into your natural nail bed, violently ripping your real nail away from the skin.

Furthermore, as your natural nail grows out over 2 to 3 weeks, the heavy acrylic gets pushed forward. The entire structural balance is ruined; the tip becomes incredibly heavy, while the back base (near the cuticle) becomes weak and hollow. 

## Our Professional Advice

To wear [Acrylic Enhancements](/prices/nails) long-term without catastrophically destroying your biological nail bed, you must abide by incredibly strict mechanical physics and hygiene rules. 

### 1. Act Like You Don't Have Them
You must fundamentally change how you interact with the physical world. 
- You cannot use the tips of your acrylics to pry open a stubborn soda can, vigorously dial a phone, or scrape a sticker off a box. Period. 
- You must always rely on the pads of your fingers or your knuckles. Using a two-inch stiletto acrylic as a lever will result in the acrylic snapping your natural nail aggressively in half, which is a brutally painful medical emergency.

### 2. Never Ignore a "Lift"
If you notice the back edge of the acrylic (near the cuticle) has lifted slightly off the natural nail, it is a severe biohazard.
- **The Danger:** When an acrylic lifts, a tiny microscopic pocket forms between the fake nail and your real nail. Whenever you wash your hands, warm water heavily plunges into that dark pocket and becomes permanently trapped. This dark, warm, wet environment is the ultimate breeding ground for Pseudomonas (the notorious "green nail" bacteria).
- If your nail lifts, do not glue it back down (which traps the bacteria inside forever!). You must immediately return to the salon to have the lifted segment cleanly filed out.

### 3. Strict 3-Week Fills
Do not try to stretch your acrylics to 5 weeks to save money.
- As the heavy acrylic grows forward, the "apex" (the thickest, strongest structural arch of the nail) moves dangerously close to the tip. This creates catastrophic leverage. If you bump that top-heavy nail, the leverage will easily rip the entire nail entirely off your finger. You must get a "fill" exactly every 3 weeks so the technician can safely re-balance the apex back to the base.

### 4. Constant Lipid Lubrication
Dry acrylic becomes brittle and snaps.
- You must apply heavy Jojoba cuticle oil specifically around the edges and entirely underneath the free edge of the acrylic every single night. The moisture prevents the surrounding skin from hardening and keeps the structural bond flexible enough to endure minor shocks.
