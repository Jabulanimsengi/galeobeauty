## Understanding How To Get Long-Lasting Nails

The frustration of an expensive manicure drastically chipping or violently peeling completely off within four days is universal. However, polish failure is rarely an issue with the brand of the polish itself. Almost all premature manicure failure is a direct result of improper adherence biology.

Your natural nail plate is composed of tightly packed keratin cells. Crucially, your nail plate naturally secretes sebum (oil) and continuously holds a specific percentage of water. 

Any polymer you place on the nail—whether it is standard lacquer, gel polish, or heavy acrylic—requires a completely bone-dry, entirely oil-free, perfectly textured surface to physically "grip" onto. If there is a microscopic layer of invisible oil, water, or dead cuticle tissue left on the nail plate before the polish is applied, the polish will inevitably lose its chemical grip and violently peel off in massive sheets.

## Our Professional Advice

If you want your [Manicures](/services/nails) to consistently last a flawless 3 to 4 weeks without a single edge lifting, you must adhere rigidly to the following biological and environmental protocols.

### 1. The Invisible Enemy: The Cuticle
The most common cause of peeling at the base of the nail is polishing over "invisible cuticle."
- The cuticle is not just the thick band of skin at the base of the nail; it is a microscopic, translucent film of dead skin that aggressively grows tightly attached to the actual nail plate. If the technician does not meticulously scrape this microscopic tissue off during prep, the gel polish is essentially painted on top of dead skin, not the nail plate. As the dead skin inevitably flakes away, it takes the polish with it.

### 2. Absolute Dehydration Before Application
You cannot paint a wet surface.
- Before any base coat touches the nail, the plate must be violently scrubbed with pure Acetone or a chemical dehydrator. Do not touch your nails, rub your face, or eat food after the dehydration step. A single molecular drop of oil from your fingerprint will cause the polish to completely reject the nail bed.

### 3. Cap the Free Edge
The tips of your nails bear 100% of the mechanical trauma of typing, scratching, and opening items.
- It is absolutely mandatory that your technician "caps the free edge." This means pulling the polish over the very front tip of the nail and painting the underside edge slightly. This encases the tip in a solid seal, preventing water and environmental shock from getting under the polish and lifting it upward.

### 4. Hydration *After* the Manicure
Once the polish is securely cured, the protocol immediately flips from extreme dehydration to extreme lubrication.
- You must slather the nails in pure cuticle oil twice a day. The oil penetrates the microscopic gaps around the rigid polish, keeping the natural nail beneath highly flexible. If the natural nail dries out and shrinks, it pulls violently away from the rigid gel on top, causing massive delamination.
