## Understanding The Difference Between Curly and Coily Hair

While "curly" is often used as an umbrella term by the general public to describe anything that isn't bone-straight, professional stylists distinguish heavily between different textures because their structural care requirements are vastly different.

The primary difference between **Curly hair (Type 3)** and **Coily hair (Type 4)** comes down to the shape of the follicle, the circumference of the curl, and how the strands interact with one another.

### Curly Hair (Type 3)
Type 3 hair forms defined loops. If you pull a single strand of Type 3 hair out, it will resemble the shape of a stretched-out spring or a distinct "S" shape. These curls usually have a circumference ranging from the size of a sidewalk chalk piece (3A) down to the size of a Sharpie marker (3C). They clump together easily when wet to form thick, defined ringlets, but are highly susceptible to frizz the moment they hit humidity.

### Coily Hair (Type 4)
Type 4 hair, often referred to as afro-textured hair, forms extremely tight, dense coils. Instead of large, bouncy "S" ringlets, coily hair strands often form an incredibly tight "Z" zig-zag pattern. The circumference of a coil is usually the size of a crochet needle—or even smaller. Because the coils are so tight and tiny, they do not clump easily into large ringlets without heavy styling gel.

## Our Professional Advice

You cannot treat Coily hair with a Curly routine, and vice-versa. Understanding your exact type dictates the entire chemical structure of the products you must purchase.

### 1. The Moisture Dilemma
- **Curly Hair:** Sebum (scalp oil) can eventually make its way down the "S" shaped spirals. Curly hair needs hydration, but heavy butters (like pure Shea) will violently weigh the "S" curls down, pulling them completely flat and stringy. Curly hair requires lightweight milks, gels, and mousses.
- **Coily Hair:** Sebum cannot navigate the microscopic tight "Z" angles of Coily hair. The ends are intrinsically starving for lipids. Coily hair absolutely requires heavy protective butters, dense creams, and thick sealing oils (like Castor oil) to survive without snapping.

### 2. Shrinkage Expectations
- **Curly Hair** usually experiences about 20% to 40% shrinkage when it dries. 
- **Coily Hair** packs so tightly upon drying that it can easily experience up to 75% shrinkage. If you have coily hair, your hair might appear ear-length when dry, but stretch all the way down your back when wet.
