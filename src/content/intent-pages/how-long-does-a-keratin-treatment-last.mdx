## Understanding How Long Does A Keratin Treatment Last

A Keratin Treatment is a semi-permanent smoothing procedure designed to eliminate massive amounts of frizz, significantly reduce styling time, and impart intense, glass-like shine to the hair. 

Unlike Japanese perms or chemical relaxers that permanently alter the internal disulfide bonds of the hair, a Keratin treatment functions as a highly durable, artificial shell. The stylist infuses liquid keratin deep into the porous gaps of the hair cuticle, and then literally "bakes" it in using a 450°F flat iron. 

Because it is an exterior shell rather than a permanent internal structural change, this artificial coating progressively washes away over time. Under optimal conditions, a professional salon [Keratin Treatment](/prices/hair/keratin) will last anywhere from **3 to 5 months**. 

## Our Professional Advice

The variance between a client who gets 3 months out of a treatment and a client who gets 5 months out of it relies almost entirely on at-home maintenance. Here is exactly how to dramatically extend the lifespan of your investment.

### 1. The Critical First 72 Hours
This is the most crucial period. Traditional Keratin formulations take exactly three days to fully neutralize and harden into their permanent shape.
- Do not wash your hair, get it wet in the rain, or sweat profusely at the gym.
- Do not tie your hair in a ponytail, use clips, wear a tight headband, or even tuck the hair behind your ears. 
- If your hair gets a kink or gets wet during this window, you must immediately blow-dry it and run a flat iron over the affected area to straighten it out again.

### 2. The Absolute Ban on Sulfates
Sulfates (like Sodium Lauryl Sulfate) are aggressive industrial detergents found in 90% of drugstore shampoos. If you use a sulfate shampoo, it will act like dish soap, brutally stripping the keratin coating straight out of your hair in a matter of weeks.
- You must exclusively use professional-grade, **sulfate-free and sodium chloride-free** shampoos.
- Remember: regular salt (sodium chloride) also degrades keratin, meaning ocean and saltwater pool swimming will prematurely strip your treatment.

### 3. Wash Less Frequently
Because the treatment washes out over time, the math is simple: the more frequently you wash your hair, the faster the keratin fades.
- Try to stretch your wash days to 2-3 times a week max. Utilize a high-quality dry shampoo on your roots in between washes to absorb excess oil without needing to wet the hair.

### 4. Limit Heat Styling
While you don't *need* a flat iron as much after a keratin treatment, using one at very high temperatures will slowly melt and degrade the keratin seal. Use a thermal protectant and stick to cooler blow-dry settings.
