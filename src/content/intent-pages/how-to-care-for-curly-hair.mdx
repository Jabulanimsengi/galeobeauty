## Understanding How To Care For Curly Hair

Curly hair is arguably the most beautiful, diverse, and voluminous hair type possible. It is also, structurally, the most complex and delicate.

The unique helical (spiral) shape of a curl occurs because the actual hair follicle inside the scalp is curved, rather than round. This structural quirk produces magnificent shape, but it immediately creates a massive vulnerability: natural sebum (the deeply moisturizing oil produced by the scalp) struggles immensely to travel down the winding shaft of a curl. 

Consequently, curly hair is fundamentally and intrinsically prone to profound dryness. Furthermore, at every "bend" or curve of the curl, the cuticle layer is slightly lifted, making curly hair highly porous, easily snagged, and desperately reactive to atmospheric humidity (frizz). 

## Our Professional Advice

Caring for curly hair means abandoning everything you were taught about standard, straight-hair routines. You must adopt a regimen devoted entirely to extreme hydration, aggressive friction reduction, and cuticle sealing.

### 1. Abandon Traditional Shampooing
Standard drugstore shampoos are loaded with Sodium Lauryl Sulfate (SLS) which creates foam but strips incredible amounts of moisture.
- **The Solution:** For heavily curly hair (Type 3 and 4), switch the majority of your washes to a "Co-Wash" (a cleansing conditioner that uses mild surfactants to break down dirt without stripping oil). 
- Only use a deeply hydrating, sulfate-free shampoo once every 10-14 days to clear away product buildup.

### 2. The Golden Rule: Only Detangle When Soaked
Running a dry brush through curly hair will immediately break the clumped curl pattern, causing the hair to explode into a massive, uncontrolled cloud of frizz, while simultaneously snapping the fragile ends.
- **The Solution:** The *only* time a comb should enter curly hair is when the hair is soaking wet, fully drenched in the shower, and heavily coated with a "slippery" deep conditioner. Always use a wide-tooth comb or a flexible detangling brush, working slowly from the tips upwards to the roots.

### 3. The LOC Moisture Method
Because curly hair is highly porous, water leaves the hair just as quickly as it enters it. You have to trap the moisture.
Immediately after washing, while the hair is still dripping wet, layer your products:
1. **L (Liquid/Leave-in):** Generously apply a hydrating, water-based leave-in conditioner.
2. **O (Oil):** Smooth a lightweight penetrating oil (like Argan or Jojoba) over the curls.
3. **C (Cream):** Finish by scrunching in a curl-defining cream or a light gel to create a "cast" that locks the moisture in and freezes the curl structure as it dries. 

### 4. Hands Off While Drying
Friction is the ultimate enemy of the curl. Do not rub your hair with a towel. Do not touch your curls while they are air-drying. If you must speed up the process, use a diffuser attachment on low heat and low airflow to cradle the curls gently until they set.
