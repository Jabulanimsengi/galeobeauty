## Understanding How To Maintain Wigs

High-quality wigs (specifically 100% human hair wigs) represent a significant financial investment, often acting as a cornerstone of protective styling and rapid aesthetic transformation. 

However, clients frequently experience a terrifyingly rapid degradation of their wigs—the hair becomes instantly matted at the nape of the neck, feels incredibly stiff like hay, or completely loses its frontal density. 

The fundamental reason human hair wigs break down so quickly is actually biological. Your natural hair is constantly fed hydrating sebum (oil) generated by the living sebaceous glands on your scalp. A wig has zero attachment to a biological scalp; it receives absolutely zero natural hydration. Every time you wash it, flat-iron it, or wear it out in the sun, you are permanently stripping it of whatever moisture the factory injected into it, resulting in rapid dehydration and macroscopic tangling.

## Our Professional Advice

To keep a wig looking flawless, flowing, and undetectable, you must treat it exactly like you would treat aggressively bleached, damaged hair. You are the sole provider of its structural hydration.

### 1. Washing: The "Plunge" Method
Violently scrubbing a wig like you scrub your real hair will instantly force the fragile lace knots to loosen and cause massive shedding, or invert the cuticles causing the wig to dreadlock instantly.
- **The Plunge:** Fill a basin with cold or lukewarm water and a squeeze of highly hydrating sulfate-free shampoo. Gently plunge the wig in and out of the water, letting the suds do the work. *Never* wring the hair out or scrub the lace.

### 2. Intense Artificial Hydration
Because the wig generates no oil, you have to force heavy lipids back into the hair physically.
- After every wash, slather the absolute lengths of the wig in a professional deep conditioning mask utilizing heavy Argan or Moroccan oil. 
- Use the **Boiling Water Method:** (Only on 100% human hair). Place the heavily conditioned wig in a robust ziplock bag and submerge the bag in hot water for 30 minutes. The extreme heat forces the cuticles open, jamming the mask inside for massive silkiness.

### 3. Protecting the Fragile Frontal
The most delicate part of any wig is the lace frontal or closure, where individual hairs are hand-tied to microscopic mesh.
- When detangling the wig, *never* yank a brush from the root through the lace. It will instantly rip the knots out, creating a massive bald spot on your expensive unit. Always secure the hair tightly in your fist, and gently brush starting from the absolute tips, working upwards.
- Avoid applying heavy lotions or conditioners directly to the knots on the lace, as this will chemically loosen the knot seal.

### 4. The Nighttime Mandate
Never, under any circumstances, sleep with your valuable wig completely loose. The friction against the pillow will cause friction matting at the incredibly dense nape of the neck. Always carefully remove the unit and place it on a mannequin head, or securely braid it and wrap it in silk before sleeping.
