## Understanding Why Hair Is Not Growing

One of the most frustrating hair experiences is feeling like your hair has been sitting at the exact same length for years. The phenomenon known as "terminal length" is often vastly misunderstood. 

While genetics do dictate a biological maximum length (determined by the duration of your Anagen growth phase), 90% of individuals who feel their hair has stopped growing are actually experiencing a mismatch between their **growth rate** and their **retention rate**.

Simply put: Your hair *is* actively growing from the scalp at the standard rate of half an inch a month. However, because the hair shaft is severely damaged, overly dry, or structurally weak, the ends are snapping and breaking off at that exact same rate of half an inch a month. Because the growth and breakage mathematically cancel each other out, it appears your hair is not growing at all.

Beyond massive breakage, actual stunted biological growth can occur due to severe stress (Telogen Effluvium), extreme nutrient deficiencies, hormonal imbalances (such as thyroid disorders), or severe scalp inflammation.

## Our Professional Advice

If you want to finally push past your invisible length barrier, you must pivot your focus from the roots of your hair to aggressively protecting the oldest and most fragile part: the ends.

### 1. Identify and Stop the Breakage
The hair tips are the oldest part of the structure. If your hair is shoulder-length, those tips are roughly two years old and have survived two years of brushing, washing, and heat styling.
- **Turn Down the Heat:** Daily flat-ironing or blow-drying is the fastest way to vaporize internal moisture, causing the tips to snap. Try heatless styles during the week and only use hot tools on a lower temperature settings alongside a high-quality thermal protectant.
- **Chemical Reset:** If you are heavily bleaching your hair, you cannot achieve massive length. Consider speaking to a [Galeo Beauty Professional](/prices/hair) about transitioning to a softer balayage or root-melt that allows the lengths to recover without constant chemical overlapping.

### 2. Moisture-Protein Balance
Hair elasticity is the secret to retaining length. If hair stretches and snaps immediately, it needs moisture. If it stretches like a gummy rubber band and then breaks, it needs protein.
- Establish a consistent routine of deep hydration masks rotated with bond-repairing or light protein treatments to keep the hair shaft flexible but heavily reinforced.

### 3. The Big Chop (Or Consistent Micro-Trims)
You cannot out-condition split ends. Once a hair strand fractures, that split will travel up the shaft until the entire strand breaks away.
- Booking a [professional haircut](/prices/hair/cut-blow-medium) every 8-10 weeks to simply "dust" the edges off is the only proven method to stop split-end travel. Saccrificing 1/4 of an inch now will save you from losing 3 inches to breakage later.
