## Understanding How To Stop Hair Breakage

Hair breakage is the harsh, physical fracturing of the hair shaft. Unlike natural hair shedding—where the entire hair falls out seamlessly from the root with a tiny white bulb attached—breakage happens anywhere along the hair shaft. This results in flyaways, uneven layers, thinning ends, and the dreaded "chemical haircut".

Breakage is exclusively a symptom of compromised structural integrity. It occurs when the inner cortex of the hair is stripped of its protective cuticle layer, leaving the keratin protein framework exposed. 

The three primary culprits of hair breakage are:
1. **Chemical Trauma:** Over-processing with bleach, perms, or harsh chemical relaxers permanently breaks down the disulfide bonds in the hair.
2. **Thermal Trauma:** Utilizing flat irons or curling wands set above 180°C (350°F) literally boils the water inside the hair cortex, causing micro-explosions that blast the cuticle apart.
3. **Mechanical Trauma:** Aggressive detangling of wet hair, tight ponytails, abrasive cotton pillowcases, and heavy extensions putting tension on the root.

## Our Professional Advice

Stopping breakage requires immediate triage to reinforce the fragile strands and fundamentally altering your daily handling habits.

### 1. Perform Chemical CPR
When your hair is breaking off in chunks, the disulfide bonds are shattered. Traditional conditioners will not fix this; they will simply coat the broken hair in silicone making it temporarily soft before it snaps.
- You must invest in clinical bond-building treatments (like Olaplex or K18) that penetrate the cortex and attempt to reconnect broken polypeptide chains. 
- Book a professional restoring treatment like a [Keratin Smoothing Treatment](/services/hair/keratin) which acts like liquid scaffolding, filling in the holes of your highly porous, breaking hair to give it temporary, rigid strength.

### 2. Change How You Handle Wet Hair
Hair is at its absolute weakest when it is wet. The hydrogen bonds are temporarily broken down by water, meaning the hair can stretch up to 30% further—which usually results in it snapping entirely.
- **Never brush wet hair with a standard brush.** Only use a wide-tooth comb or a brush specifically engineered for wet detangling.
- Start detangling from the very bottom tips of your hair and slowly work your way up to the root. Ripping a brush from the root downwards forces all tangles into one massive knot at the bottom, breaking off the tips.

### 3. Low-Tension Styling
Mechanical friction is a massive contributor to daily breakage that goes unnoticed.
- Avoid painfully tight top-knots or slick-backs that put immense tensile stress on your hairline and crown. Use gentle silk scrunchies or claw clips instead of standard elastic bands.
- Sleeping on a silk pillowcase prevents the severe friction that causes the "birds-nest" tangling you usually have to rip out the following morning.
