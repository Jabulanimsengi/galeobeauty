## Understanding How To Choose A Salon

Selecting an aesthetic clinic or beauty salon is not equivalent to selecting a restaurant or a coffee shop; it is the equivalent of selecting a minor surgical partner. 

You are actively paying a stranger to apply highly toxic, rapidly polarizing chemicals (acrylic monomers, cyanoacrylate glues) adjacent to your eyes, forcefully rip hundreds of live hair follicles out of your skin, or completely chemically sever the disulfide bonds holding your actual hair together. 

The beauty industry is notoriously flooded with "discount" establishments that drastically undercut the market price. The only mathematical way a salon can offer a drastically cheap service is by heavily compromising on invisible infrastructure: specifically, the utilization of illegal, unsterilized tools; the purchase of cheap, counterfeit, or massively corrosive chemicals from the grey market; and employing entirely uncertified, untrained technicians who use high-speed brute force rather than meticulous chemistry. Choosing poorly mathematically guarantees a severe fungal infection, chemical burn, or structural hair loss.

## Our Professional Advice

You must heavily audit an establishment before allowing them to manipulate your biological architecture. At Galeo Beauty, we operate under clinical-grade standards, and we implore clients to demand this specific level of rigor everywhere.

### 1. Demand Visual Sterilization Evidence
A wiping cloth is not sanitization. 
- You must physically look for a **medical-grade Autoclave** or heavy Barbicide saturation tanks. If a nail technician simply sprays a metal cuticle nipper with alcohol and immediately uses it on your hands, run out the door. Metal implements that capable of puncturing the skin must be chemically sterilized for 15 minutes, or physically baked in an autoclave to destroy Staph bacteria and Hepatitis. 
- Furthermore, completely porous items (nail files, wooden spatulas, pumice stones) physically cannot be sanitized. If they pull a used, dusty nail file out of a drawer, leave immediately.

### 2. Audit the Chemical Supply Chain
The salon must not hide its inventory in the back.
- Heavy chemical services (Keratin, Chemical Peels, Acrylics) are highly regulated. A premium salon will always prominently display the legitimate, authorized brands they utilize (such as Dermalogica, Olaplex, or elite gel systems). If the products are trapped in unmarked, mysterious plastic tubs with the labels completely ripped off, the salon is heavily diluting the product illegally or using cheap, corrosive black-market chemicals (like MMA in nails) that violently ruin your body.

### 3. Analyze the Consultation Rigor
A professional salon does not rush you straight to the treatment chair.
- If you request a heavy [Chemical Peel](/prices/dermalogica/pro-power-peel), and the esthetician does not spend at least 10 minutes heavily auditing your at-home skincare routine, medical history, and sun exposure, they are highly negligent. True professionals demand to understand your exact dermal chemistry before applying an acid.

### 4. Ventilation is Mandatory
Your lungs are just as important as your hair.
- If you walk into a salon and the smell of harsh chemical monomer or burning hair is so violently strong that it physically burns your throat and waters your eyes, the salon lacks structural HVAC multi-filtration. Constant inhalation of trapped beauty chemicals is massively toxic.
