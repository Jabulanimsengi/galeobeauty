## Understanding How To Care For 4C Hair

Type 4C hair is the most exquisitely textured, densely packed, and intricate of all hair types. It is characterized by an extremely tight "Z" pattern, or a zig-zag coil that does not form a defined spiral. 

Because the curls are so tight and closely packed, 4C hair experiences up to 75% "shrinkage"—meaning the hair is often massively longer than it appears when dry. 

However, this tight zig-zag structure creates severe vulnerabilities. Every single "bend" in the zig-zag represents a point of structural weakness where the cuticle is slightly lifted. Furthermore, the natural oil produced by the scalp (sebum) finds it physically impossible to navigate the dense, tight coils to reach the ends. Consequently, 4C hair is naturally the driest and most fragile hair type on the spectrum, highly susceptible to snapping and macroscopic tangling if not handled with profound care.

## Our Professional Advice

Caring for 4C hair is almost surgical. It requires an absolute dedication to intensive moisture layering, protective styling, and extreme friction reduction.

### 1. The Pre-Poo Ritual
Never enter the shower with dry 4C hair. The friction of the water and shampoo will instantly cause massive tangling and breakage.
- **Pre-Poo (Pre-Shampoo):** Thirty minutes before washing, heavily coat your lengths and ends in a robust penetrating oil, like coconut or olive oil. This creates a highly lubricated "slip," protecting the fragile ends from the stripping effects of the shampoo.

### 2. Ditch The Traditional Shampoo 
Drugstore shampoos contain sodium lauryl sulfate—a harsh detergent that will absolutely destroy the fragile moisture balance of 4C hair.
- Shift to a strict **Co-washing** routine (washing exclusively with a cleansing conditioner). 
- Limit actual shampooing (using a specialized, hydrating sulfate-free formula) to only once every two weeks to clear away heavy product buildup.

### 3. The LCO Method is Non-Negotiable
You cannot just apply a moisturizer and walk away; the moisture will evaporate in under an hour. You must trap it using the LCO method while the hair is drenched:
1. **L (Liquid/Leave-in):** Apply a heavily hydrating, water-based leave-in to dripping wet sections.
2. **C (Cream):** Massage a heavy, buttery cream (like Shea or Mango butter) deep into the coils to provide heavy lipid nourishment.
3. **O (Oil):** Seal the impenetrable barrier shutting with a dense sealing oil like Castor Oil.

### 4. Protective Styling
To retain massive length in 4C hair, the ends must be tucked away from the damaging elements of daily friction (collars, cotton shirts, wind).
