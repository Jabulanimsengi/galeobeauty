## Understanding Best Oils For Hair Growth

The concept of using oils for hair growth has roots in centuries of Ayurvedic and indigenous practices. However, modern trichology (the scientific study of hair and scalp health) has brought a more nuanced understanding to how oils actually function. 

It is crucial to understand that applying oil to the ends of your hair will *not* stimulate growth from the root. Oil applied to the ends simply acts as a sealant, preventing moisture loss and protecting against split ends (which helps with length *retention*). 

To physically stimulate new growth, oils must be applied directly to the scalp. The mechanism here is twofold: 
1. The mechanical action of massaging the oil into the scalp increases blood circulation, delivering oxygen and nutrients to the hair follicles and prolonging the Anagen (active growth) phase.
2. Certain highly concentrated essential oils contain vaso-dilating and anti-inflammatory properties that optimize the follicular environment.

## Our Professional Advice

Not all oils are created equal. You must distinguish between **carrier oils** (which moisturize) and **essential oils** (which stimulate). Never apply pure essential oils directly to your scalp; they must always be diluted.

### 1. The Holy Grail: Rosemary Essential Oil
Studies have shown that Rosemary oil performs similarly to minoxidil (the active ingredient in Rogaine) for stimulating hair growth, but without the harsh side effects like scalp itching. 
- **How to use:** Mix 5 drops of pure Rosemary essential oil into a tablespoon of a lightweight carrier oil (like Jojoba or Argan oil). Massage it deeply into your scalp, leave it on for 2 hours, and then double-shampoo it out. Do this twice a week.

### 2. Peppermint Essential Oil
Peppermint oil is a potent vasodilator. When applied to the scalp, it causes a physical tingling sensation, forcing blood vessels to dilate and dramatically increasing blood flow to starving hair follicles.

### 3. The Best Carrier Oils
- **Jojoba Oil:** This is the only oil that chemically mimics the natural sebum produced by the human scalp. It is incredibly lightweight and perfect for fine hair that easily gets weighed down.
- **Castor Oil:** Exceptionally thick and rich in ricinoleic acid. While it is famous for eyelash and eyebrow growth, it is extremely dense. We recommend using it sparingly on the scalp or mixing it with a lighter oil, as it can be difficult to wash out.

**A Professional Warning:** If you suffer from seborrheic dermatitis or severe dandruff, oiling your scalp can actually feed the yeast that causes the condition, making your hair loss much worse. In these cases, focus on clinical scalp exfoliation rather than heavy oiling.
