## Understanding Stubborn Belly Fat Treatment

When attempting to treat severe or stubborn abdominal, it is biologically mandatory to understand exactly what *type* of fat is structurally plaguing the area. The human abdomen holds two completely biologically distinct forms of adipose tissue. 

**Visceral Fat** is the highly dangerous, hard fat that is entirely trapped deep beneath your abdominal muscle wall. It wraps directly around your vital organs (liver and intestines). Because visceral fat is trapped behind a wall of solid muscle, it physically cannot be pinched from the outside, and it makes the stomach protrude outward like a hard, tight drum. Visceral fat cannot be removed via Liposuction, Fat Freezing, or any cosmetic procedure. It answers strictly to intense cardiovascular exercise and caloric restrictions.

**Subcutaneous Fat** is the soft, jiggly, squishy fat that sits outside the muscle wall, resting directly beneath the physical skin. This is the fat that overflows a waistband and can be easily grabbed and squeezed with two hands. Because stubborn subcutaneous fat is entirely superficial and localized, it is the absolute perfect, highly lucrative target for intense Cryolipolysis (Fat Freezing) destruction.
