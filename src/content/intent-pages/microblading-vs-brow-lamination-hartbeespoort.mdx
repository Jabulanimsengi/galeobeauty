## Microblading (The Semi-Permanent Illusion)

Microblading is an invasive dermal implantation process. We use a manual hand-tool fitted with a row of microscopic needles to slice fine, linear channels into the upper dermis. Pigment is then deposited into these channels to mimic the appearance of individual eyebrow hairs. 

Because Microblading is technically a tattoo, it involves a "Wound Healing" phase. Over the first 14 days, the skin will scab and flake as it encapsulates the pigment. It is the ultimate solution for clients with **Alopecia or heavy scarring**, as it creates the illusion of hair where none exists biologically. However, it requires a permanent commitment and a touch-up every 12 to 18 months to prevent the pigment from blurring.

## Brow Lamination (The Painless Fluff)

Brow Lamination is a chemical re-texturizing treatment—essentially a "perm" for your eyebrows. We use a high-alkaline lifting cream to break the disulfide bonds in the hair shaft, allowing us to physically redirect the hair into a more vertical, upward position. We then neutralize the hair to "lock" it in this new, voluminous shape.

This treatment maximizes the vertical surface area of your existing hair. If your brow hairs naturally point downward or lay flat, lamination will make your brows appear 50% thicker instantly without a single needle. It is the gold standard for the [Brushed-Up 'Soap Brow' Look](/services/lashes-and-brows).

## The Commitment Factor

Before choosing [Microblading](/services/lashes-and-brows), consider the biological evolution of your face. As we age, our brow bone naturally descends. A tattooed brow that looks perfect today may look saggy or out of place in ten years. Lamination, however, is a non-permanent intervention that lasts 6 to 8 weeks before your hair returns to its natural state. This allows you to follow current beauty trends without the risk of a "permanent mistake" on your face.
