## Why melasma is different from ordinary dark marks

Not all pigmentation behaves the same way. Some marks are left behind after acne, while melasma tends to behave more like a recurring pattern that can flare with sun exposure, heat or hormonal shifts.

That is why many people find that brightening serums help a little, but do not fully solve the problem.

## When a pigmentation-focused treatment makes the most sense

If the main concern is visible dark patches or uneven tone, the first conversation is usually around options designed specifically for pigment. At Galeo, [Melanopro Peel](/services/dermalogica/melanopro-peel) and [Pro Power Peel](/services/dermalogica/pro-power-peel) are the treatments people most often compare when looking for a stronger next step.

## Where microneedling fits

[Pro Microneedling](/services/dermalogica/pro-microneedling) can still be part of a broader skin plan, especially when texture or overall skin quality also matter. But if the main frustration is melasma itself, pigment-focused treatment is usually the more direct place to begin.

## Why consistency matters so much

Melasma treatment is rarely about one dramatic session. It usually works better as a controlled plan that combines the right in-clinic treatment with ongoing sun protection and realistic expectations about maintenance.

## A better first move

If you are dealing with dark patches and are unsure whether they are melasma, post-acne marks or general uneven tone, it usually makes more sense to start with assessment and a matched treatment plan than to jump straight into the strongest peel you can find.
