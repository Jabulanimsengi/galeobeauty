## Understanding Keratin vs Brazilian Blowout

When looking for a semi-permanent solution to eliminate frizz, cut down blow-dry time, and smooth out unruly textures, two names dominate the salon industry: Keratin Treatments and Brazilian Blowouts. While they are often used interchangeably by clients, they are fundamentally different treatments with different chemical mechanisms, application methods, and end results.

A **Keratin Treatment** is primarily a smoothing and restructuring process. It utilizes liquid keratin (the protein your hair is made of) to fill in the microscopic gaps in your hair's cuticle. It focuses heavily on deep conditioning, making the hair extremely heavy, straight, and densely packed.

A **Brazilian Blowout**, on the other hand, relies on a liquid amino-acid polymer system. Rather than filling the hair shaft with dense protein, the Brazilian Blowout creates a customizable, protective sealant around the exterior of the hair strand.

## Our Professional Advice

Choosing between the two depends entirely on your hair type and your desired outcome. Here is how Galeo Beauty professionals break down the decision for our clients:

### 1. The Desired Texture and Volume
If you have extremely thick, unruly, coarse hair and your goal is to make it as absolutely pin-straight and flat as possible, you want a [Keratin Treatment](/prices/hair/keratin). The heavy protein molecules weigh the hair down, eliminating volume.

If you have fine hair, wavy hair, or curls that you simply want to be completely frizz-free *without* losing your natural volume and bounce, you should opt for a [Brazilian Blowout](/prices/hair/brazilian). The Brazilian Blowout allows you to keep your natural texture (or wear it straight if you choose) while completely bullet-proofing it against humidity.

### 2. The Downtime
One of the most significant differences lies in post-treatment care. Traditional Keratin deeply penetrates and "cures" over three days. During this 72-hour window, you cannot wash your hair, sweat profusely, tie it up, or even tuck it behind your ears without risking a permanent dent.

A Brazilian Blowout is fully cured the moment you leave the salon chair. You can immediately wash your hair, go to the gym, or tie it up in a ponytail without any repercussions.

### 3. Longevity and Fade-Out
Both treatments are semi-permanent and slowly wash out over time.
- **Keratin:** Lasts 3 to 5 months. As it washes out, it can sometimes leave a slight line of demarcation where your natural untreated roots meet the heavily treated mid-lengths.
- **Brazilian Blowout:** Lasts about 12 weeks. It fades out entirely evenly across the hair shaft, meaning you will never experience a harsh line between new growth and treated hair.
