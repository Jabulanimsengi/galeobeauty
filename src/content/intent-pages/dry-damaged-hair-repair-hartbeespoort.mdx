## Understanding Dry & Damaged Hair Repair

Dry hair and damaged hair are often used interchangeably, but they are biologically distinct pathologies. **Dry hair** is a lipid deficiency; your scalp is not producing enough sebum, or the hair's protective cuticle has been slightly raised, allowing essential moisture to evaporate. **Damaged hair** is a structural collapse; the internal disulfide bonds that provide the hair's strength have been chemically or thermally snapped.

When you subject your hair to intense chemical bleaching or daily 230°C flat-ironing, you are not just "drying it out." You are physically melting the keratin proteins and creating "craters" in the hair shaft. Once the internal cortex is exposed, the hair loses its elasticity, meaning it will snap like a dry twig rather than stretching when pulled.

To truly repair damaged hair, we cannot simply coat it in heavy oils. We must chemically re-engineer the internal bonds and physically seal the cuticle to lock the new structure in place.

### 2. Protein vs. Moisture Balance
Do not overdose on protein. **The Rule:** If your hair feels mushy, gummy, and stretches forever before snapping, you lack **protein**. You need a Keratin-heavy treatment. If your hair feels rough, crunchy, and snaps immediately, you have too much protein and lack **moisture**. You need heavy lipids like Argan or Jojoba oil. 

### 3. The Cold Rinse Mandate
Heat opens the hair; cold seals it. **The Protocol:** Always execute your final rinse with the coldest water you can tolerate. Cold water causes the cuticle scales to lay perfectly flat and tight against the hair shaft, trapping your clinical treatments inside and reflecting light for a high-gloss finish.

### 4. Thermal Defense is Non-Negotiable
If you use heat without protection, you are sabotaging the repair. A high-quality thermal shield creates a microscopic chemical barrier that absorbs the heat energy before it can melt your keratin. Applying a flat-iron to bare, unprotected hair is the fastest way to induce irreversible damage.
