## Understanding How Long Does Hair Botox Last

Hair Botox is universally incredibly popular because it achieves massive restorative results without utilizing the harsh aldehydes or relaxing chemicals found in traditional smoothing treatments. Rather than acting as a chemical straightener, it functions as an ultra-concentrated filling plumper for your hair strands.

Because [Hair Botox](/prices/hair/botox) is fundamentally a deep-conditioning collagen and vitamin infusion (rather than a structural chemical change), its longevity works on a curve. Unlike bleached color that has to physically grow out to disappear, Hair Botox acts as an internal moisturizing deposit that gradually dissipates every time you wash your hair, sweat profusely, or swim in chlorinated water.

On average, a professional in-salon Hair Botox treatment will last exactly **2 to 4 months**. 

## Our Professional Advice

Whether you fall on the 2-month end of the spectrum or the 4-month end depends overwhelmingly on two factors: the porosity of your hair *before* you received the treatment, and the frequency with which you wash it *after*.

### 1. The Porosity Paradox
If your hair was severely damaged, heavily bleached, or "gummy" before the treatment, your hair cuticles are essentially wide open. While Hair Botox will beautifully fill all those broken holes and restore the elasticity, those open cuticles also mean the treatment will slide right back out much faster than it would on healthy, low-porosity hair. 
- *Pro Tip:* If your hair is heavily damaged, your very first Hair Botox treatment might only last 6 weeks. However, because each treatment builds upon the last, consecutive sessions will eventually push your longevity past the 3-month mark.

### 2. Washing Habits Dictate Lifespan
This is the single biggest deciding factor. If you wash your hair every single day, you will strip the collagen complex out of your hair in less than a month, regardless of the shampoo you use.
- **Stretch Wash Days:** You must stretch your wash schedule to 2 to 3 times a week maximum. 
- **Dry Shampoo:** Strategically use dry shampoo on your roots on day 2 or 3 to absorb oils without needing to undergo a full wet-wash that strips the mids and ends.

### 3. The Strict Product Ban
Just exactly like a Keratin Treatment, utilizing a cheap drugstore shampoo containing **Sodium Lauryl Sulfate (SLS) or Sodium Chloride (Salt)** will violently foam up and strip the Hair Botox out of the cortex immediately. 
- You must invest in a sulfate-free, deeply hydrating salon-grade shampoo to ensure the longevity of the service.
- If you swim in the ocean (saltwater) or a pool (chlorine), always drench your hair in fresh tap water and coat it in a light leave-in conditioner before jumping in. This prevents your porous hair from aggressively soaking up the damaging pool water!
