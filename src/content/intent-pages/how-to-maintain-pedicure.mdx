## Understanding How To Maintain A Pedicure

A clinical, professional pedicure is significantly more than just applying polish to the toes; it is an active restoration of the foot's structural biology. The esthetician painstakingly removes impacted cuticles, heavily exfoliates the dead keratin bonds on the sole, and properly trims the nail plate straight across to prevent severe ingrown toenails.

Because the feet are constantly encased in dark, damp socks and aggressively pounded against the insides of shoes for 10 hours a day, they endure arguably the most hostile environment on the human body. 

A freshly pedicured foot looks incredibly smooth and pristine in the salon, but if subjected to immediate friction, trapped moisture, or profound dehydration upon returning home, the results will rapidly deteriorate. The beautiful gel polish will chip, the heels will immediately re-harden, and the cuticles will rapidly overgrown to defend the nail base.

## Our Professional Advice

To significantly prolong the pristine, buttery-soft results of a [Professional Pedicure](/prices/pedicures), you must aggressively manage the environment inside your shoes and provide daily lipid maintenance.

### 1. Control the Damp Environment
Fungus and bacterial infections thrive in the dark, hot, moist environment between your toes.
- **The Rule:** You absolutely must thoroughly dry the physical spaces *between* your toes after every single shower. Do not simply step on a bath mat. Trapped water between pedicured toes causes the skin to macerate (turn white and mushy) and destroys the impeccable salon cuticle work. 
- Always wear highly breathable, moisture-wicking socks to prevent the buildup of heavy sweat that degrades gel polish and breeds bacteria.

### 2. The Nightly Cuticle Command
Your toenails grow significantly slower than your fingernails, meaning the cuticle has more time to tightly adhere to the nail plate.
- **The Routine:** To keep the polish looking fresh and the nail bed looking elongated, you must rub a heavy cuticle oil (like Jojoba) into the base of each toenail every single night. This keeps the skin surrounding the nail incredibly soft and prevents the cuticle from drying out and forcefully climbing up the gel polish.

### 3. Maintain the Heel Polish
The esthetician removed your heavy calluses, but the friction of walking will immediately signal your body to rebuild them.
- Do not let the dead skin aggressively pile up for a month. Twice a week, while the skin is heavily saturated in the shower, use a gentle pumice stone to lightly "buff" the heels. This micro-maintenance prevents the massive keratin buildup that leads to aggressive cracking.

### 4. Footwear Dictates Polish Longevity
If you leave a pedicure appointment and immediately shove your feet into incredibly tight, pointed-toe heels or stiff running shoes, the intense mechanical friction will violently rub against the toenail edge, causing premature chipping. 
- Ensure your daily footwear has a wide enough toe box that your toes are not physically smashing into each other or rubbing constantly against the top of the shoe.
