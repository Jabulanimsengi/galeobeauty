## Understanding Frizzy Hair

Frizz occurs when the cuticle layer of your hair is raised, allowing moisture from the air to pass into the hair shaft, causing it to swell and lose its smooth structure.

Think of your hair cuticle perfectly resembling overlapping shingles on a roof. When hair is healthy and hydrated, those shingles lie flat, reflecting light (creating shine) and locking out excess humidity. However, when the hair is dry, damaged, or genetically highly porous, those shingles lift up. In humid environments, the dry hair desperately reaches out to absorb moisture from the surrounding air, resulting in the chaotic, swollen manifestation we call frizz.

Curly and wavy hair types are naturally highly prone to frizz because the structure of the curl makes it difficult for the scalp's natural oils to travel down the hair shaft, leaving the lengths inherently drier than straight hair.

## Our Professional Advice

Fighting frizz successfully requires a combination of deep hydration, cuticle sealing, and utilizing treatments that offer long-term humidity shielding.

### 1. Professional Smoothing Treatments
If you spend hours fighting frizz with hot tools only to have it puff up the moment you step outside, a professional treatment is your ultimate solution.
- **Keratin Treatments:** A [Keratin Smoothing Treatment](/prices/hair/keratin) infuses liquid keratin deep into the cuticle, essentially spackling the raised gaps and sealing the hair shut. This drastically reduces frizz and blow-dry time for up to 3-4 months without permanently altering your natural curl pattern.
- **Brazilian Blowouts:** Similar to keratin, a [Brazilian Smoothing Treatment](/prices/hair/brazilian-medium) creates a protective protein layer around the hair shaft to eliminate frizz and smooth the cuticle, offering incredibly sleek results tailored to your specific hair type.

### 2. Rethink Your Washing Routine
Water temperature and the mechanics of washing play a huge role in frizz.
- Start washing with lukewarm water and finish your condition with a **cool rinse**. Cold water drastically helps lay the cuticle "shingles" flat before you step out of the shower.
- Stop rough-drying your hair with a standard terrycloth towel. The harsh loops create massive friction. Instead, gently squeeze excess water out with an old cotton t-shirt or a microfiber hair towel.

### 3. Anti-Humectant Styling Products
Hydration is great, but when you step into a humid environment, you need an anti-humectant to block the weather out.
- Incorporate a lightweight silicone or heavy-duty oil serum onto the mid-lengths and ends of your hair *after* styling. This creates a waterproof barrier around the hair shaft, stopping the atmospheric moisture from penetrating and swelling the hair.
