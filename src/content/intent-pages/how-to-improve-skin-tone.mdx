## Understanding How To Improve Skin Tone

"Skin Tone" is the catch-all term for the overall color uniformity and luminosity of your complexion. Uneven skin tone (Dyschromia) is caused by the erratic behavior of melanocytes—the cells responsible for producing pigment. When these cells are triggered by UV light, hormones, or old inflammation, they begin to dump melanin in disorganized clumps, resulting in a mottled, dull, or splotchy appearance.

To improve skin tone, we must execute a dual-front attack: we must chemically strip away the existing surface clumps of pigment while aggressively poisoning the enzyme (Tyrosinase) that tells your skin to produce new spots.

### 3. The 365-Day SPF Mandate
Pigment has a biological "memory." Even a 3-minute walk to your car in the sun without protection will re-trigger the exact same dark spots we worked for months to fade. To maintain an even skin tone, consistent application of broad-spectrum SPF 50 is non-negotiable every single day, regardless of the clouds.
