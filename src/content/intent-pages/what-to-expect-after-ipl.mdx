## Understanding What To Expect After IPL

An IPL (Intense Pulsed Light) treatment intentionally inflicts massive controlled thermal trauma directly into your dermal layer. For the first few hours directly following the treatment, your face has essentially sustained a severe, highly targeted, internal sunburn.

Because the treatment is entirely non-ablative (meaning it does not cut open the skin or cause physical bleeding), clients often naively assume they can immediately return to the gym or take a boiling hot shower. This is a highly dangerous assumption. 

The heat injected by the IPL machine remains trapped deep under the skin for up to 48 hours. The blood vessels are severely dilated, and the shattered pigment clusters are highly vulnerable as the body begins the complex biological process of pushing them toward the surface for shedding. Any external heat, friction, or harsh chemicals introduced during this volatile window will hyper-stimulate the trauma, potentially turning a safe clinical treatment into horrible post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (scarring).

## Our Professional Advice

After leaving the [treatment room](/services/dermalogica), the biological "healing" phase begins. Managing the extreme heat within the skin while protecting the shedding process is crucial for flawless results.

### 1. The Immediate Sensation (The Intense Sunburn)
Do not panic when you look in the mirror two hours later.
- It is entirely medically normal for your face to feel like it is radiating intense heat and to visually appear flushed, inflamed, and bright red (similar to a severe sunburn). This intense erythema usually completely subsides within 4 to 12 hours. Do not apply ice packs directly to the skin, as the violent temperature swing can cause shock; instead, use a cool, damp cloth gently laid over the face.

### 2. The 48-Hour "No Heat" Lockdown
Your skin is currently holding excessive heat. If you add more heat, you will boil the tissue.
- **The Protocol:** For exactly 48 hours after IPL, you must completely avoid hot showers, saunas, hot tubs, heavy cardiovascular exercise (sweating), and even sitting next to a roaring fireplace. Any internal or external temperature spike will violently exacerbate the vasodilation, making the redness last for days and potentially triggering traumatic pigmentation. 

### 3. The "Coffee Ground" Shedding Phase
If you received IPL for brown sun spots, prepare for the spots to look drastically worse before they look better.
- Around 24 to 48 hours after treatment, the shattered brown melanin will forcefully push to the surface of your skin, looking like incredibly dark, crusty scabs or "coffee grounds." 
- **The Absolute Rule:** You must never, under any circumstances, pick, scratch, scrub, or aggressively exfoliate these dark flakes off. If you forcefully rip a scab off before the skin underneath is fully healed, you will create a permanent, deep, white scar (hypopigmentation). Let the flakes wash off naturally in the shower over the next 10 days.

### 4. Aggressive SPF Shielding
Your newly treated skin is hyper-sensitive to the sun.
- If you walk outside without heavy SPF within the first two weeks of an IPL session, UV radiation will violently attack the vulnerable, recovering cells, instantly bringing the dark spots right back. You must apply a broad-spectrum, physical SPF 50 every single morning and wear a wide-brimmed hat.
