## How to tell which acne treatment you actually need

The word "acne" covers a few different problems. If your skin is red, sore and actively breaking out, the priority is to calm inflammation. If your skin is mostly blocked with blackheads, bumps and oil, the bigger job is deep decongestion. If the breakouts have improved but the marks remain, then the plan shifts away from calming and toward correction.

That is why the best first booking is not always the strongest treatment on the menu. It is the one that matches the stage your skin is in now.

## The treatments we most often use for acne-prone skin

### 1. [Pro Clear](/prices/dermalogica/pro-clear) for inflamed, breakout-prone skin

This is usually the strongest first fit when your skin feels oily, congested and reactive. The focus is on clearing the pore environment, reducing visible inflammation and helping the skin feel less overloaded.

### 2. [Skin Clearing Facial](/prices/dermalogica/skin-clearing-facial) for blackheads and congestion

If your main frustration is clogged pores, rough texture and recurring congestion, a dedicated clearing facial is often the more practical starting point. It is especially useful when your skin needs a reset without immediately jumping into a more corrective treatment.

### 3. [Pro Power Peel](/prices/dermalogica/pro-power-peel) when marks and texture become the bigger issue

Once the active breakouts are under better control, a peel can help with lingering post-acne marks, dullness and uneven texture. It is usually not the first step for angry, irritated acne, but it can become a strong next step later.

### 4. [Pro Microneedling](/prices/dermalogica/pro-microneedling) for shallow scarring and post-acne texture

Microneedling is more of a corrective treatment than a clearing one. We normally consider it after the skin is calmer and the main concern is texture, shallow acne scarring or roughness left behind.

## A simple way to choose your first booking

- If your skin is sore, inflamed and actively breaking out, start with [Pro Clear](/prices/dermalogica/pro-clear).
- If you mostly see blackheads, blocked pores and oil, start with [Skin Clearing Facial](/prices/dermalogica/skin-clearing-facial).
- If the breakouts are fading but the marks are hanging on, ask about [Pro Power Peel](/prices/dermalogica/pro-power-peel).
- If your concern is more about shallow scarring or acne texture than active acne, ask whether [Pro Microneedling](/prices/dermalogica/pro-microneedling) is the right next phase.

## When it makes sense to book professional help

If you keep changing products, your skin improves for a week and then relapses, or you are stuck between breakouts and marks without knowing what to do first, that is usually the point where treatment becomes more useful than more guesswork. A focused plan tends to save time, product waste and unnecessary irritation.
