## Understanding How To Fix Damaged Hair

Damaged hair is rarely the result of a single event. It is usually the cumulative effect of chemical processing (like bleaching or relaxing), excessive heat styling, mechanical friction, and environmental exposure. When hair becomes damaged, the protective outer layer—the cuticle—lifts and fractures. This exposes the inner cortex, allowing vital moisture to escape while leaving the structural integrity of the hair compromised.

You will know your hair is deeply damaged when it exhibits **high porosity** (absorbing water rapidly but drying out just as fast), feels "gummy" or stretchy when wet, snaps easily when brushed, or refuses to hold a style.

There is a distinct difference between dry hair and damaged hair. Dry hair simply lacks moisture and sebum, and can be relatively easily fixed with deep conditioning. Damaged hair, however, has suffered structural protein loss. To fix it, you have to temporarily rebuild that structure while supporting healthy new growth.

## Our Professional Advice

While you cannot biologically "heal" dead hair strands, you can drastically improve their appearance, texture, and strength to prevent further breakage while the healthier hair grows out.

### 1. Rebuild with Protein (But Don't Overdo It)
Your hair is made primarily of keratin protein. When it is damaged, it has literal holes in its structure.
- **Keratin Treatments:** A professional [Keratin Treatment](/prices/hair/keratin) acts like liquid filler, smoothing over the fractured cuticles and temporarily rebuilding the hair shaft for up to 3-4 months.
- **At-Home Bond Builders:** Incorporate a bond-building treatment into your routine once a week. These products cross-link broken bonds inside the cortex rather than just coating the outside.

### 2. Deep Hydration
Protein makes hair strong, but moisture makes it flexible. Too much protein without moisture will cause hair to snap.
- Always follow a protein or bond-building mask with a deeply emollient hydration mask. Look for ingredients like Argan oil, Shea butter, and hyaluronic acid.

### 3. Pause Chemical Processing
The hardest but most necessary step.
- Suspend all bleaching, lifting, or harsh chemical relaxers. If you absolutely must color your hair, consult with a [Galeo Beauty Professional](/prices/hair) about using demi-permanent glosses or toners that deposit color and shine without lifting the cuticle.

### 4. Severe Damage Requires the Scissors
Split ends are like a run in a stocking; if you do not cut them off, they will travel up the hair shaft and break off higher up.
- A "dusting" or micro-trim every 6 weeks is mandatory. Getting a [professional haircut](/prices/hair/cut-blow-medium) removes the unsalvageable ends, preventing the damage from spreading and immediately making the hair look dramatically thicker.
