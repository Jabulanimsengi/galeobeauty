## Understanding What Is A Keratin Treatment

A Keratin Treatment is a revolutionary semi-permanent chemical service performed in premium salons to dramatically smooth the hair cuticle, eliminate up to 95% of frizz, and add mirror-like shine. 

To understand how it works, you have to understand the anatomy of human hair. Your hair is composed of nearly 90% keratin protein. When hair is subjected to chemical processing, environmental UV damage, or daily heat styling, that natural keratin depletes, leaving microscopic "potholes" in the hair shaft. This makes the hair porous, frizzy, and fragile.

During a professional [Keratin Treatment](/services/hair/keratin), a stylist paints a highly concentrated liquid keratin formula onto the hair. The hair is then blow-dried and meticulously passed over with a titanium flat iron set to 450°F (232°C). The extreme heat triggers a chemical reaction that effectively bakes the artificial keratin into the "potholes" of your hair, sealing the cuticle tightly shut.

## Our Professional Advice

A Keratin treatment is arguably one of the most life-changing services for clients with thick, unruly hair. However, it requires a significant financial investment and specific maintenance to ensure longevity.

### 1. It is a Smoothing Treatment, Not a Straightener
Traditional Keratin is designed to make your hair immensely sleek and cut down your blow-dry time by roughly 60%. While it will drastically loosen your natural curl pattern and make the hair *appear* much straighter, it does not permanently break the disulfide bonds like a Japanese straightening perm. If you have tight curls, you will still have a wave after washing, but you will not have any frizz.

### 2. The Strict 72-Hour Rule
If you opt for a traditional Keratin complex, the bonding process does not finish in the salon chair. It takes 3 full days to neutralize.
- You absolutely cannot wash your hair for 72 hours.
- You cannot put your hair in a tight ponytail, use clips, or even tuck it behind your ears, or you risk setting a permanent dent into the cooling keratin structure.
- If your hair gets wet from rain or sweat during this window, you must immediately blow-dry and flat-iron the damp section.

### 3. Mandatory Sulfate-Free Maintenance
Because Keratin is semi-permanent, it essentially acts like a protective shell that gradually washes away over 3 to 5 months.
- Washing your hair with a standard drugstore shampoo containing Sodium Lauryl Sulfate (SLS) is like washing your car with dish soap; it will aggressively strip the costly keratin straight out of your hair in a few washes. You must switch to a professional, sulfate-free and sodium-chloride-free shampoo.
