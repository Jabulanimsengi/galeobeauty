## Sun damage is usually more than one issue

For many clients, sun damage is not only about brown spots. It can also mean rougher texture, uneven tone, skin that looks more tired than it used to, and fine lines that feel more visible after years of exposure.

That is why the best treatment depends on what is standing out most now.

## When pigment is the main concern

If age spots, patchiness or uneven tone are the main frustration, a peel or depigmentation-focused option is usually the more direct place to start. At Galeo, [Melanopro Peel](/services/dermalogica/melanopro-peel) and [Pro Power Peel](/services/dermalogica/pro-power-peel) are the types of treatments people usually ask about when they want to address visible pigment changes.

## When texture and skin quality matter more

If the skin feels rough, less resilient or generally more weathered rather than just pigmented, [Pro Microneedling](/services/dermalogica/pro-microneedling) may become more relevant. That is usually the option considered when the focus shifts toward collagen support and overall skin quality.

## Why prevention still matters

Corrective treatment helps, but sun protection remains part of the treatment plan, not an optional extra. If the skin keeps taking on more UV stress, pigmentation and photoageing can keep returning regardless of how good the corrective treatment is.

## How to choose a first step

- Start with a pigmentation-focused option if the main issue is spots or uneven tone.
- Ask about microneedling if texture and firmness are the bigger concern.
- If you are not sure which layer of sun damage is driving the problem most, start with an adaptable treatment or consultation rather than guessing.
