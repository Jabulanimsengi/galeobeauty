## Understanding How To Maintain Lash Extensions

Eyelash extensions are an incredibly delicate architectural structure. A skilled technician spends two hours meticulously isolating your natural biological lashes and attaching synthetic fibers to them using tiny, microscopic drops of medical-grade cyanoacrylate adhesive.

This adhesive creates a hard, rigid acrylic capsule around the base of your natural lash. 

Because extensions are glued to your natural hair, their lifespan is entirely dictated by your body's natural "lash cycle." A human typically sheds between 2 to 5 natural eyelashes every single day. When the natural lash falls out, the extension goes with it. 

However, premature extension loss—when massive "chunks" of extensions fall out within the first week—is almost entirely caused by client negligence. If you expose the rigid acrylic adhesive to chemicals that break it down, or mechanical forces that violently yank the extension, the bond shatters. 

## Our Professional Advice

Maintaining eyelash extensions requires you to dramatically alter how you wash your face, how you sleep, and what cosmetic products you use. Here is the strict Galeo Beauty retention protocol.

### 1. The Total Eradication of Oil
Oil is the ultimate enemy of cyanoacrylate lash adhesive. 
- Oil molecules seamlessly slip under the rigid glue capsule, violently degrading the structural integrity of the bond until the synthetic fiber effortlessly slides straight off your natural lash. 
- **The Rule:** You must throw away all oil-based makeup removers, heavy cleansing balms, and oil-heavy eye creams. You must exclusively use "Lash-Safe" or water-based/gel cleansers around your eye architecture.

### 2. Washing is Non-Negotiable
A massive, dangerous myth is that "water ruins extensions." Clients avoid washing them, leading to catastrophic biological failure. 
- Your eyelids produce sebum (natural oil) and trap dead skin cells. If you do not wash your lashes, this oil and dead skin builds up into a thick crust at the base of the extensions. This greasy crust rots the glue and causes severe Blepharitis (eyelid bacterial infection). 
- **The Rule:** You absolutely must wash your extensions every single night using a specialized foaming lash cleanser and a soft, fluffy cleansing brush. Gently stroke the foam downwards over the closed eyes to remove perfectly remove the daily oil buildup.

### 3. The Friction Protocol
You cannot rub your eyes like you used to.
- If you physically rub your eyes with your knuckles, the massive mechanical force will violently snap the extension off, often taking your real lash with it. 
- If you sleep violently on your stomach, smashing your face into a cotton pillowcase, the friction will pull the outer corner lashes entirely out. You must train yourself to sleep on your back or use a hollow-cup sleeping mask.

### 4. Book Your Fills Militantly
Extensions do not last forever. Because your natural lashes grow out, the heavy extension gets pushed farther away from the eyelid. Eventually, the extension becomes top-heavy, causing your natural lash to violently twist and break.
- You must pre-book your [Lash Fill Appointment](/services/lashes-and-brows) strictly every 2 to 3 weeks. The technician will manually remove the dangerous, "grown-out" extensions and re-balance the architecture with fresh fibers.
