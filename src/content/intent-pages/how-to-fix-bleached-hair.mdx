## Understanding How To Fix Bleached Hair

Bleaching your hair is the single most aggressive chemical procedure you can subject your hair to. To forcefully lift dark pigment out of the hair shaft, bleach relies on high-volume alkaline developers that violently blast the hair cuticle wide open, plunge into the cortex, and shatter the natural melanin molecules.

While this achieves stunning blondes, the collateral damage is massive. The oxidative stress permanently shatters the disulfide bonds (the internal scaffolding that gives hair its strength) and destroys the lipid barrier. 

Consequently, heavily bleached hair becomes "hyper-porous." When wet, it feels entirely hollow, gummy, and stretchy like old chewing gum. When dry, it feels stiff, stringy, and looks entirely opaque with zero shine. It cannot hold onto moisture because the cuticle flaps are permanently stuck open.

## Our Professional Advice

You cannot "heal" bleached hair; it is completely dead tissue whose internal architecture has been blown apart. However, you can dramatically reinforce it, add artificial scaffolding, and significantly improve its visual health while it grows out.

### 1. The Necessity of Bond Builders
Standard conditioners are completely useless on freshly bleached, gummy hair. They just coat the fragile strand in heavy silicones.
- You must use clinical bond-building treatments (like Olaplex No.3 or K18). These specific molecules are engineered to penetrate the shattered cortex and temporarily cross-link the broken disulfide bonds back together, giving the hair rigid strength so it does not snap off when you brush it.

### 2. Protein vs. Moisture Paramedic Routine
Bleached hair needs both protein to fill the holes and moisture to provide slip. But if you get the balance wrong, the hair will break.
- **Add Protein:** Use a keratin or hydrolyzed protein mask twice a month to temporarily fill the massive potholes in the cuticle. 
- **Add Moisture:** Follow *every single protein treatment* immediately with a heavy, deeply hydrating lipid mask containing Argan or Marula oil. The protein makes the hair hard and strong; the moisture makes it flexible so it doesn't snap.

### 3. Toning is Non-Negotiable
Because the hair is hyper-porous, it will aggressively absorb environmental pollution, hard water minerals, and smoke, turning your icy blonde into a brassy, muddy yellow within weeks.
- Utilize a high-quality purple shampoo once a week to neutralize yellow tones. 
- Book a professional [Color Gloss or Toner](/prices/hair/colour-short) every 6 weeks. A gloss uses a highly acidic, zero-lift formula that not only deposits beautiful color but physically forces the blown-open cuticle to snap shut, locking in incredible shine.

### 4. Cold Turkey on the Flat Iron
You cannot flat-iron gummy, bleached hair. The residual moisture inside the fragile structure will instantly boil, causing the hair to quite literally melt off. You must rely on heatless styling and massive doses of leave-in conditioners to manage the texture.
