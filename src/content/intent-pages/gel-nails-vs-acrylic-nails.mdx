## Understanding Gel Nails Vs Acrylic Nails

The choice between Gel and Acrylic is the most fundamental architectural decision you can make in a nail salon. They are not merely different types of "polish." They are completely separate chemical compounds that harden (polarize) through entirely different scientific mechanisms.

**Acrylic Nails** are structurally built using a two-part chemical system: a liquid monomer and a powder polymer. When the nail technician dips the brush into the liquid and then into the powder, it creates a fast-drying, dough-like bead. This bead is aggressively sculpted over your natural nail (and often a plastic extension tip). Crucially, acrylic cures (hardens) entirely on its own through ambient air exposure within minutes. Once dry, acrylic is incredibly rigid, rock-hard, and practically a plastic shield over your nail.

**Gel Nails** (referring to true hard gel or soft builder gel, not just gel polish) arrive pre-mixed in a thick, honey-like consistency. Gel will *never* dry in the ambient air. It remains completely wet and malleable until it is physically thrust under a specialized UV or LED lamp. The intense UV light triggers a violent photo-initiator reaction, instantly curing the gel into a solid matrix. Gel is structurally different than acrylic because it retains a microscopic degree of flexibility, allowing it to bend slightly with your natural nail.

## Our Professional Advice

Both systems are incredibly durable, but they cater to entirely different natural nail beds and lifestyle habits. Here is how Galeo Beauty technicians prescribe the perfect enhancement.

### 1. The Rigidity Factor (Who needs Acrylic)
Acrylic is the undisputed champion of brute strength. 
- Because acrylic cures into a rigid, inflexible rock, it is mathematically necessary for clients who violently use their hands for heavy labor, or for clients who want drastically, unnaturally long stiletto extensions. 
- However, if your natural nails are paper-thin and highly flexible, applying a rock-hard acrylic over them can be disastrous. If you jam your finger, the acrylic will not bend; it will violently rip your natural nail straight off the nail bed.

### 2. The Flexibility Factor (Who needs Gel)
Gel is molecularly designed to mimic the natural flexibility of a biological keratin nail.
- When you bang a [Gel Nail enhancement](/prices/nails) against a steering wheel, the enhancement slightly bends to absorb the shock, rather than snapping. This makes Gel the absolute superior choice for clients with naturally weak, brittle nails who want a natural-looking overlay to protect the nail as it grows out. 

### 3. The Scent and Toxins
If you are highly sensitive to chemical odors, the decision is made for you.
- Liquid monomer (used in acrylics) emits a pungent, intensely strong chemical odor that permeates the entire salon. Hard gel is entirely odorless. For pregnant clients or those with severe migraines, Gel is the only chemically comfortable option.

### 4. The Removal Process
You must understand how you plan to take them off.
- Standard acrylics and soft gels can be legally soaked off in 100% pure acetone. The acetone chemically melts the bonds down to a mush.
- **True Hard Gel**, however, is entirely impervious to acetone. It physically cannot be soaked off. It must be meticulously and professionally filed completely off with an electronic drill by a master technician.
