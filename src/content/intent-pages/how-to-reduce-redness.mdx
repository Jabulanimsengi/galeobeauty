## Understanding How To Reduce Redness

Chronic facial redness (Erythema) is a sign of a compromised skin barrier or over-active vascular activity. Biologically, redness occurs when the microscopic capillaries near the surface of the skin dilate to rush blood to an area of perceived trauma or inflammation.

If your skin is constantly red, you likely have a "shattered" acid mantle. When your barrier is thin, environmental triggers (like heat, wind, or spicy food) can easily irritate the underlying nerves and blood vessels. To [Reduce Redness](/prices/dermalogica), you must stop using aggressive scrubs and focus entirely on **Vasoconstriction and Barrier Repair**.

## Our Professional Advice

Treating redness requires a cooling, anti-inflammatory protocol designed to "calm" the skin's nervous system.

### 1. The Cooling "Facial Ice" Protocol
During a [Calming Facial](/prices/dermalogica/pro-skin-60) at Galeo Beauty, we utilize specialized cooling globes and ultra-soothing gels containing **Oat Kernel and Aloe Vera**. These ingredients act as biological "extinguishers," instantly dropping the skin's temperature and signaling the dilated capillaries to constrict, which flat-lines the redness immediately.

### 2. Barrier Lipid Replacement
Redness is often a symptom of "thirsty" skin. We utilize concentrated **Ceramide and Panthenol serums**. These molecules act as liquid mortar, filling the holes in your skin barrier. When the barrier is thick and healthy, environmental irritants cannot reach the blood vessels, preventing the "flushing" response before it starts.

### 3. Avoiding the "Niancinamide Flush"
While Niacinamide is great for oil, high concentrations can actually cause a "flush" in sensitive individuals. For redness-prone clients, we pivot toward **Azelaic Acid**. This clinical ingredient is famous in the dermatological world for its ability to reduce chronic redness and calm the inflammatory markers that cause Rosacea-like symptoms.
