## Understanding Keratin vs Hair Botox

While they both sound like medical procedures for your hair, Keratin Treatments and Hair Botox serve entirely different therapeutic purposes in the salon suite.

A **Keratin Treatment** involves utilizing heat (usually a 450-degree flat iron) to seal cross-linking formaldehyde-releasing chemicals or heavy protein complexes into the hair violently restructuring its shape. It violently eliminates frizz and artificially straightens the hair.

**Hair Botox** is completely chemical-free and contains no actual Botulinum Toxin (Botox). It earned its name because of how it functions: it behaves identically to how facial botox smoothing out wrinkles by deeply plumping the hair. Hair botox is an intensive, hyper-concentrated deep conditioning mask infused with caviar oil, B5, E vitamins, and collagen complex. It plumps up the hair cuticle from the inside out, restoring vitality and youthfulness to heavily aged or bleached hair.

## Our Professional Advice

If you are torn between these two services at the salon, your decision should be based not on how you want your hair to *look*, but rather on the current *biological health* of your hair.

### 1. When to Choose Keratin
Opt for a [Keratin Treatment](/services/hair/keratin) if your hair is thick, unmanageable, heavily textured, or impossible to blow-dry, but is fundamentally healthy enough to withstand the extreme 450-degree heat required to seal the keratin in. 
- *Goal:* Straightening, extreme frizz reduction, cutting styling time in half.
- *Warning:* If your hair is currently snapping off or heavily bleached, the flat-ironing process of a Keratin treatment will destroy your hair.

### 2. When to Choose Hair Botox
Opt for [Hair Botox](/services/hair/botox) if your hair is severely compromised, heavily bleached, thinning, breaking off, or excessively dry. 
- Hair Botox contains zero relaxing chemicals. It is purely restorative. It will *not* straighten your curls; it will just make them the shiniest, most hydrated version of themselves.
- *Goal:* Reversing massive damage, restoring elasticity, and plumping the hair strand so it feels thicker.

### 3. Can I have both
Yes, but never on the same day. Many of our clients at Galeo Beauty utilize Hair Botox as a "rehabilitation" program for a few months to restore the internal structure of their bleached hair, before eventually moving on to a Keratin treatment once the strands are strong enough to handle it.
