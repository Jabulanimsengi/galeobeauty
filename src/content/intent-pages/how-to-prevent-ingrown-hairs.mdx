## Understanding How To Prevent Ingrown Hairs

An ingrown hair is a structural, biological failure of the hair follicle's exit trajectory. 

When a hair is removed from the root (via waxing or tweezing) or violently chopped off at the surface (via shaving), the body immediately begins regenerating a new tip. Under normal circumstances, this hair grows straight up the follicular canal and flawlessly exits the pore. 

An ingrown hair occurs when a microscopic barrier (usually a heavy buildup of dry, dead skin cells or hardened sebum) acts like a concrete lid over the exit pore. The fragile, growing hair hits this concrete lid, realizes it cannot push through, and physically curls backward. It begins violently growing horizontally *underneath* the skin. The body’s immune system detects this trapped hair as a highly dangerous foreign object and launches a massive inflammatory attack, resulting in deep, painful, red swelling filled with pus.

## Our Professional Advice

Treating a painful ingrown hair is a miserable process of lancing and extraction. The ultimate goal is proactive environmental manipulation: creating a skin surface so soft, clear, and unobstructed that an ingrown hair mathematically cannot form. 

### 1. Destroy the "Concrete Lid" (Chemical Exfoliation)
Physical loofahs are often not strong enough to remove the microscopic glued layer of dead skin cells blocking the pore.
- **The Routine:** You must introduce chemical exfoliation to your body routine. Utilize a strong **Salicylic Acid (BHA)** or **Glycolic Acid (AHA)** body wash or leave-on serum twice a week over areas prone to ingrowns (like the bikini line or neck). The acid chemically incinerates the dead skin cells blocking the exit route, dissolving the "concrete lid" completely before the new hair ever reaches the surface.

### 2. Abolish the Dull Razor
If you are violently shaving over the same area five times with a dull, rusty three-blade razor, you are guaranteeing ingrown hairs.
- A dull blade does not cut the hair cleanly; it pulls the hair violently above the skin, slices it diagonally, and snaps it back *below* the skin level. 
- You must throw away disposable razors after 3 uses, and only shave using thick, heavy shaving cream to eliminate blade friction. Or better yet, abandon shaving entirely and transition to [Professional Waxing](/prices/waxing-and-tinting), which removes the blunt edge completely.

### 3. Hyper-Hydration (Make the Skin Pliable)
A new hair is extremely fine and fragile. It cannot push through a barrier of dry, leathery skin.
- You must deeply moisturize the skin immediately after showering while the pores are violently open. Using a heavy, occlusive lotion makes the stratum corneum (the top layer of skin) incredibly soft and elastic, allowing the fragile new hair to slip effortlessly through the surface without buckling and turning inward.

If an ingrown hair has already formed a deep, massive cyst, do NOT dig into your skin with unsanitary bathroom tweezers. You will cause a severe staph infection and a permanent black scar. Book a clinical extraction with a Galeo Beauty esthetician.
