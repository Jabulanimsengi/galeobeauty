## Understanding How To Protect Hair At Night

For 8 hours every single night, you are entirely unconscious while your hair is subjected to massive, relentless mechanical trauma. If you are struggling to grow your hair past a certain length or constantly battling massive "birds-nest" tangles every morning, your nighttime routine is almost certainly the culprit.

As you toss and turn in your sleep, your hair rubs against the fabric of your bedsheets. If you sleep on standard cotton pillowcases, the fibers act like microscopic sandpaper against the delicate cuticles of your hair. This friction violently lifts the cuticle, physically ripping the moisture out of the hair shaft and causing the strands to aggressively latch onto one another in massive tangles.

Furthermore, if you sleep with your hair loose, the weight of your body pressing down on the ends as you move creates immense tensile stress, causing the older, fragile ends of your hair to simply snap off.

## Our Professional Advice

Protecting your hair at night is a non-negotiable requirement for retaining length and eliminating morning breakage. Our master stylists recommend implementing the following nightly "armor" routine.

### 1. The Silk or Satin Mandate
You must immediately throw out your cotton pillowcases.
- Cotton is inherently highly absorbent, meaning it literally drinks the expensive hydrating serums straight out of your hair while you sleep.
- **The Solution:** Invest in 100% silk or high-quality satin pillowcases. Silk provides effortless "slip." When you toss and turn, the hair simply glides across the surface, resulting in zero friction, zero cuticle lifting, and zero morning tangles. 

### 2. Never Sleep on Wet Hair
Going to bed with wet hair is one of the most disastrous things you can do to your hair's structural integrity.
- When hair is wet, the hydrogen bonds inside the cortex are temporarily broken, meaning the hair can stretch up to 30% further. This makes it incredibly elastic—and incredibly fragile.
- If you toss and turn on hyper-elastic wet hair, it will stretch until it snaps. Furthermore, wet hair trapped against a warm scalp all night creates the perfect breeding ground for fungal dandruff. Ensure your roots are 100% bone-dry before your head hits the pillow.

### 3. The Low-Tension Pineapple or Braid
Never sleep with your hair completely loose, as it will inevitably wrap around itself or get trapped underneath your shoulders.
- **For Straight/Wavy Hair:** Sweep your hair into a very loose, low braid at the nape of your neck, and secure it with a soft silk scrunchie. This locks the lengths together, preventing them from catching and tangling.
- **For Curly/Coily Hair:** Utilize the "Pineapple" method. Flip your head upside down and gather all your curls into an extremely loose, high ponytail right at the top front of your head, using a silk scrunchie. This prevents you from physically crushing your precious curl pattern while you sleep on your back or side!
