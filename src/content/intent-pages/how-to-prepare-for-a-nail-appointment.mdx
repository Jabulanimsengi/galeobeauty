## Understanding How To Prepare For A Nail Appointment

The longevity and structural integrity of a professional manicure—whether it is incredibly rigid acrylic enhancements or flexible soft-gel overlays—depends entirely on the concept of microscopic adherence. 

Nail enhancements do not mechanically "snap" onto your fingers. They rely on complex acrylic or cyanoacrylate chemistry to create an intense, permanent bond with the keratin layers of your natural nail plate. For this bond to successfully hold for 3 to 4 weeks under the immense mechanical stress of daily life, your natural nail plate must be flawlessly dry, entirely free of microscopic fluid, and completely devoid of organic oils.

Clients unknowingly sabotage their own [Manicures](/services/nails) before they even walk through the salon doors by engaging in behaviors that heavily saturate the nail plate with water or lipids. 

## Our Professional Advice

If you want your nail enhancements to be virtually indestructible without premature lifting, you must perfectly prepare the biological canvas. Here is the strict Galeo Beauty pre-appointment protocol.

### 1. The Water Embargo (No Swimming or Long Baths)
Your nail plate naturally functions like a highly porous sponge.
- If you take a massive, 45-minute hot bath or go swimming directly before your appointment, your nails will dramatically swell with retained water. The nail technician will paint the gel over this swollen nail. Over the next 24 hours, the water evaporates out of your nail, causing the keratin to shrink back to its normal size. The rigid gel polish on top cannot shrink with it, instantly causing massive, violent peeling in giant sheets. Keep your hands completely dry for 2 hours before the appointment.

### 2. Abolish Pre-Appointment Lotion
Oil is the ultimate enemy of the acrylic bond.
- Do not beautifully slather your hands in thick, heavy coconut oil or rich hand cream under the assumption that you are being "polite" to the technician. The heavy oil molecularly plunges into the microscopic grooves of the nail plate. The gel polish will sit on top of the oil, entirely failing to grip the keratin. Arrive with incredibly dry, completely bare, un-lotioned hands.

### 3. Do Not Trim Your Own Cuticles
Unless you hold an active professional cosmetology license, step away from the metal cuticle nippers.
- When clients attempt to aggressively hack away at their own cuticles, they almost universally slice directly into the living eponychium tissue. This causes rapid, localized inflammation and bleeding. A nail technician is legally forbidden from applying heavy nail chemicals over an open, bleeding wound due to the extreme risk of a Staph infection. Let the professional perform the dry Russian manicure prep.

### 4. Know Your Desired Architecture
Do not sit at the table with zero concept of what you are purchasing.
- Nail geometry is highly specific. Understand the fundamental difference between heavy structural Acrylic (for immense length) and Soft Builder Gel (for flexible natural overlay) before sitting down, as they require completely different structural prep work.
