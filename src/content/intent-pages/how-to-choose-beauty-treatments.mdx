## Understanding How To Choose Beauty Treatments

The aesthetic market is currently saturated with thousands of highly technical, aggressively marketed clinical treatments. Without a fundamental understanding of your own biological architecture—specifically your skin type, metabolic rate, and inflammatory baseline—selecting a beauty treatment based purely on an Instagram trend is mathematically dangerous.

Treatments are strictly divided into specific, opposing biological mechanisms. Some treatments **ablate** (destroy tissue to force regeneration, like Microneedling or Chemical Peels). Other treatments **nourish and occlude** (saturate the skin with lipids and oxygen, like Hydrafacials). Other treatments **polarize structures** (like Laser Photothermolysis or Keratin hair bonding).

If you have highly compromised, incredibly thin, Rosacea-prone skin, and you blindly choose a harsh, ablative 30% TCA Chemical Peel because an influencer recommended it, you will physically incinerate your stratum corneum, resulting in second-degree chemical burns. Choosing a treatment is a clinical diagnosis, not a casual shopping experience.

## Our Professional Advice

You must reverse-engineer the selection process. Never start with the *name* of the treatment. Always start with the specific biological *problem* you need to eradicate. Book a [Professional Consultation](/prices/dermalogica) at Galeo Beauty to have a master esthetician map your dermal needs.

### 1. Identify the Core Pathology
Do not focus on "glowing skin." Focus on what is *stopping* the glow.
- Is your core issue textural (rough bumps, massive pores, pitted acne scars) You require a structural, resurfacing treatment like **Microneedling** or Microdermabrasion to physically break down the uneven landscape.
- Is your core issue pigment (dark brown sun spots, red vascular broken capillaries) You require a color-targeting treatment like **IPL (Intense Pulsed Light)** to violently shatter the melanin without breaking the skin surface.

### 2. Assess Your Inflammatory Tolerance
Are you prone to keloid scarring Do you turn bright red when someone just touches your face
- If your skin is highly reactive (sensitive), you physically cannot sustain ablative trauma. You must select highly soothing, barrier-repairing treatments focused on LED Light Therapy, cold-therapy, and massive lipid infusion.

### 3. Calculate Your Downtime Reality
Clinical biology takes time to heal.
- If you have thirty days before your wedding, do not book a heavy chemical peel. Your skin will violently shed like a snake for 14 days, and you will not be able to wear heavy makeup. Be violently honest with your esthetician about your social calendar so they can select a treatment with zero social downtime (like Dermaplaning) if necessary.

### 4. Let the Professional Decide
The greatest mistake a client makes is arguing with the esthetician's diagnosis.
- You are paying for their clinical mastery. If they refuse to perform aggressive microdermabrasion on your active, bleeding cystic acne (because it will spread the Staph infection across your entire face), you must trust their anatomical knowledge. They will prescribe a completely different, enzyme-based treatment that chemically digests the acne without physical friction.
