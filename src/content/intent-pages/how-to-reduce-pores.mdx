## Understanding How To Reduce Pores

Biologically, it is impossible to "close" your pores. Pores are not like doors; they are simply the openings of your hair follicles. However, you can drastically reduce their *appearance* by addressing the three factors that cause them to look enlarged: **Congestion, Elasticity Loss, and Oil Production.**

When a pore is filled with hardened oil (blackheads), it physically stretches the follicular opening, making it appear wider. Furthermore, as we age, we lose the collagen "scaffolding" that holds the pore tightly in place. Without this support, the pore sags and appears as a large, visible hole. To [Reduce Pores](/services/dermalogica), we must clear the congestion and tighten the surrounding dermal matrix.

## Our Professional Advice

To achieve a "filtered" skin appearance, we focus on structural tightening and chemical regulation.

### 1. Chemical Resurfacing (Smoothing the Edge)
We utilize **Niacinamide (Vitamin B3)** and **Retinol**. These clinical actives speed up cellular turnover, ensuring that dead skin cells do not accumulate around the rim of the pore. When the edges of the pore are smooth and flat, light reflects off them uniformly, making them virtually invisible to the naked eye.

### 2. Dermal Tightening with Microneedling
To fix "sagging" pores caused by aging, we utilize [Microneedling](/services/dermalogica/pro-microneedling). By triggering the body's natural collagen production, we "re-stuff" the dermal layer. This increased volume pushes the walls of the pores closer together, physically shrinking their visible diameter from the inside out.

### 3. Oil Production Management
The more oil your skin produces, the larger your pores must be to transport that oil to the surface. By utilizing **Clay Masks** and **Oil-Free Hydrators**, we signal the sebaceous glands to slow down. When oil production is balanced, the pores sit in a relaxed, minimized state.
