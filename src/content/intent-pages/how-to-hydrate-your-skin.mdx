## Understanding How To Hydrate Your Skin

Hydrating your skin is not the same as moisturizing it. Moisturizing is about **Lipids** (oils) and repairing the skin's external barrier. Hydrating is about **Water** (H2O) and increasing the actual fluid content within the skin cells. You can have incredibly oily skin that is simultaneously severely dehydrated.

When your skin is dehydrated, it loses its "scaffolding" and becomes crepey, dull, and prone to fine lines. The epidermis begins to develop microscopic cracks, allowing "Transepidermal Water Loss" (TEWL). This creates a vicious cycle where your skin becomes increasingly tight and inflamed, leading to premature aging.

## Our Professional Advice

Mastering [Skin Hydration](/services/dermalogica) requires a two-step process: flooding the dermis with fluid and then "locking the door" to keep it there.

### 1. Hyaluronic Acid Infusion (The Sponge)
Hyaluronic Acid is a biological superhero that can hold up to 1000 times its own weight in water. However, most retail HA serums use molecules that are too large to sink in. We utilize **Nanoinfusion** technology to press medical-grade, multi-weight Hyaluronic Acid deep into the tissue, effectively turning your skin into a water-dense sponge.

### 2. Closing the Barrier (TEWL Prevention)
You cannot fill a bucket that has holes in the bottom. Once we have infused the hydration, we must apply an **Occlusive** layer. These are specific high-quality oils (Squalane or Ceramides) that act like a microscopic lid over your skin. This lid physically blocks water from evaporating into the air, keeping your skin plump and dewy for days.

### 3. Internal Osmosis
Drink with intention. While topical hydration is critical, your skin is the last organ to receive the water you drink. If you are chronically dehydrated internally, your body will steal water *from* your skin to feed your vital organs. Consume a minimum of 2.5 liters of water daily to support your clinical topical treatments.
