## Understanding How To Take Care Of Natural Hair

"Natural hair" typically refers to Black or afro-textured hair (Type 3C to 4C) that has not been structurally altered by chemical relaxers or texturizers. It is the most chemically pure state of the hair, but it requires the most immense dedication to its hydration architecture.

The structure of natural coils relies entirely on a tight helical (spiral or zig-zag) pattern. While this provides spectacular, gravity-defying volume, it introduces the ultimate biological hurdle: sebum (the scalp's deeply moisturizing natural oil) simply cannot travel down the twists and angles of the hair shaft. 

Therefore, the mid-lengths and boundaries of natural hair exist in a continuous state of profound dryness. Furthermore, the coils naturally interlock into one another, creating an environment where macroscopic tangling and mechanical snapping can happen rapidly if not handled with exceptional care and deep lipid-level moisture integration.

## Our Professional Advice

Caring for natural hair is exclusively about moisture retention, gentle manipulation, and executing protective practices that guard the ends of the hair from physical friction.

### 1. Master the L-C-O Method
Natural hair is incredibly porous; water evaporates from the strand almost instantly. Applying a single moisturizer will not work. You must trap the moisture while the hair is completely soaked.
1. **L (Leave-In/Liquid):** Immediately after washing, aggressively apply a water-based leave-in conditioner to soaking wet hair. 
2. **C (Cream):** Massage a heavy, deeply moisturizing cream or thick butter (like Shea, Mango, or Cocoa) directly into the curls. This represents the primary heavy lipid layer.
3. **O (Oil):** You must seal the butter. Run a heavy sealing oil (like Jamaican Black Castor Oil or pure Argan oil) over the absolute exterior of the curls to lock the trapdoor shut. 

### 2. Radical Reduction of Shampooing
Standard shampoos containing sodium lauryl sulfate are disastrous for natural hair; they violently strip what little natural lipid barrier exists on the curl.
- You must transition to a strict **Co-Wash** routine (cleansing with a specialized low-lather hydrating conditioner). 
- Limit actual shampooing (and only use professional, zero-sulfate hydration shampoos) to once every 10 to 14 days solely to remove heavy butter buildup from the scalp.

### 3. High-Tier Protective Styling
Retaining massive length means completely hiding the fragile ends from friction against coats, shirts, and dry winter air.
- Employ low-tension protective styles: two-strand twists, knotless braids, or bantu knots. These styles lock the moisture into the strand and prevent the coils from rubbing together and snapping. 
- Be extremely careful with heavy extensions or tight synthetic braids; if installed with too much tension, the pulling force will cause severe, permanent traction alopecia at the hairline.
