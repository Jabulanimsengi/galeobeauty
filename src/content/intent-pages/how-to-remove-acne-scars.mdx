## Understanding How To Remove Acne Scars

Acne "scars" are often misunderstood. Most clients are actually suffering from **Post-Inflammatory Hyperpigmentation (PIH)**—dark red or brown spots left behind after a blemish heals. True acne scarring, however, involves structural depressions in the skin (pockmarks) caused by a loss of collagen during a severe infection.

Biologically, clearing these marks requires two separate approaches. For dark spots, we must speed up cellular turnover to "wash" the pigment away. For structural pits, we must trigger the body's **Wound Healing Response** to manufacture brand-new collagen fiber to "fill" the hole from the bottom up.

## Our Professional Advice

At Galeo Beauty, we utilize clinical-grade protocols to resurface the skin and erase the memory of past breakouts.

### 1. Chemical Resurfacing (For Dark Spots)
We utilize high-concentration **Glycolic and Lactic Acid peels**. These acids aggressively dissolve the top layers of the skin where the excess pigment is trapped. By forcing the skin to shed these layers, the dark spots fade significantly faster than they would through natural exfoliation alone.

### 2. Microneedling (For Pitted Scars)
For structural "ice-pick" or "boxcar" scars, topical creams are essentially useless. We recommend [Professional Microneedling](/services/dermalogica/pro-microneedling). This device creates thousands of microscopic "micro-injuries" in the scar tissue. The body responds by flooding the area with growth factors and creating a fresh matrix of collagen and elastin, physically lifting the scar to be level with the surrounding skin.

### 3. Vitamin C & Retinol Synergism
Your homecare is the "insurance" for your professional treatments. You must use a stabilized **Vitamin C serum** in the morning to prevent the spots from darkening further, and a **Retinol** at night to keep the cells in a constant state of regeneration.
