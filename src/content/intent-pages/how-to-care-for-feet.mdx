## Understanding How To Care For Feet

The skin on the bottom of your feet operates completely differently than the skin on your face. Biologically, the soles of your feet (the plantar surface) lack sebaceous (oil) glands entriely. They physically cannot naturally lubricate themselves. 

Furthermore, because humans bear their entire body weight on their feet daily, the skin builds up massive, hyper-thickened layers of keratinized tissue (calluses) simply as a mechanical defense mechanism against friction. 

When you combine a biological inability to produce oil with a massive accumulation of hard, dead skin, the result is severe dehydration. This dehydration eventually progresses into deep, extremely painful fissures (cracked heels) where the thick, brittle callus physically snaps under the pressure of walking, tearing the live, sensitive skin underneath.

## Our Professional Advice

Treating your feet requires aggressive exfoliation simultaneously combined with hyper-lipid occlusion. A simple lightweight body lotion will not penetrate the callus barrier.

### 1. Safely Remove the Defense Barrier (Callus Reduction)
You must remove the thick layer of dead skin, but you must do so strategically.
- **The Warning:** Never violently shave off calluses with those extremely dangerous "cheese grater" razors. If you remove 100% of the callus in one session, your body panics, senses a massive lack of protection, and will grow the callus back twice as thick and twice as hard within a week.
- **The Routine:** Invest in a professional glass pumice stone or a fine diamond-grit foot file. While in the shower, gently file the heels for just 60 seconds. Do this three times a week to slowly and consistently polish away the hard edges without triggering the body's over-production defense.

### 2. The Acid Exfoliation Hack
If physical filing is not enough, you must use chemistry.
- Consider utilizing a **Keratolytic Cream** containing high percentages of Urea (10% to 40%), Lactic Acid, or Salicylic Acid. These potent chemical exfoliants slowly dissolve the intracellular glue holding the thick callus together, chemically melting the hard skin away while you sleep.

### 3. Maximum Occlusion (The Sock Method)
Because your feet cannot produce oil, you must artificially manufacture a heavy lipid barrier every night.
- **The Routine:** Every single night before bed, slather your feet in an incredibly dense, heavy ointment (like Shea Butter or pure petroleum jelly). Immediately slide on a pair of 100% cotton socks. The socks trap your body heat, melting the heavy ointment down and violently driving the moisture deep into the tough skin while preventing the oil from wiping off onto your sheets.
