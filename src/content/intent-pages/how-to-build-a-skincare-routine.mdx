## Understanding How To Build A Skincare Routine

Building a skincare routine is not a matter of piling on as many trendy products as your face can legally hold. Biologically, the skin operates as a semi-permeable fortress. Its primary job is to actively *prevent* foreign compounds from entering your bloodstream. 

When you apply a chaotic cocktail of poorly formulated serums, heavy creams, and acidic toners entirely out of order, you cause molecular traffic jams. Heavy lipids will block lightweight serums from penetrating, while opposing pH levels will literally neutralize expensive active ingredients, rendering them entirely useless.

A functional, clinical skincare routine is an architectural sequence. It must follow the strict laws of molecular weight and chemical synergy. A successful routine strips away environmental debris, forcefully injects clinical actives into the cellular matrix, and completely seals the chemical reaction under a heavy occlusive barrier.

## Our Professional Advice

If you have a cabinet full of products and have absolutely no idea what order to apply them in, you must adhere to the "Thinnest to Thickest" rule. Products with smaller molecular structures (like watery toners) cannot penetrate through heavy molecular structures (like thick creams). 

Here is the non-negotiable [Dermalogica-approved](/prices/dermalogica) framework for building a fundamental routine.

### Step 1: The Cleanse
You must remove environmental pollution, excess sebum, and SPF barrier creams before any treatment can enter the pore.
- **Morning:** A simple, gentle splash of water or a very mild gel cleanser. Do not over-wash in the morning; your skin is not dirty from sleeping.
- **Night:** You must "Double Cleanse." Use an oil-based cleanser first to literally melt away waterproof sunscreen and heavy foundation, followed immediately by a foaming gel cleanser to wash the broken-down sludge down the drain.

### Step 2: The Treatment (Serums & Toners)
This is where you deploy the active clinical chemicals to solve your specific concern (acne, wrinkles, or dullness).
- Apply watery essences or toners immediately after washing while the skin is damp (never use alcohol-based astringents). 
- Follow very strictly with a targeted serum: **Vitamin C** in the morning (to act as a shield against microscopic free radicals and pollution), and **Retinol or Peptides** at night (to aggressively stimulate cellular turnover while you sleep).

### Step 3: The Moisture Seal
Even the most expensive hydrating serum in the world is useless if you do not seal it. Water naturally evaporates out of the skin into the air (Transepidermal Water Loss).
- You must apply a moisturizer (a lighter lotion for oily skin, a dense cream containing ceramides for dry skin) to act as a physical lid, violently locking the treatment serums and massive moisture inside the skin barrier.

### Step 4: The Ultimate Protection (Morning Only)
Every preceding step is entirely pointless if you skip this. 
- You must slather your face in broad-spectrum **SPF 30 (or higher) sunscreen** every single morning, rain or shine. UV radiation is the singular cause of 90% of premature aging, severe dark spots, and the fundamental destruction of facial collagen.
