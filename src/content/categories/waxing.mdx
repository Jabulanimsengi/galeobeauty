## How to Care for Your Skin Before and After Waxing

A little prep and proper aftercare can make your waxing experience much more comfortable and prevent annoying ingrown hairs.

### Before Your Waxing Appointment
- **Let it Grow:** The hair needs to be at least a quarter of an inch long (about the size of a grain of rice) for the wax to grip it effectively. This usually means 3 to 4 weeks of growth after your last shave or wax.
- **Exfoliate Gently:** Gently exfoliate the area a day or two before your appointment to remove dead skin cells and help release any trapped hairs. Do not scrub harshly on the day of your appointment.
- **Skip the Lotion:** Come to your appointment with clean, dry skin. Do not apply any lotions, oils, or deodorants to the areas being waxed, as this can prevent the wax from adhering properly.

### After Your Waxing Appointment
- **Keep it Cool and Clean:** Your pores will be open and susceptible to bacteria. Avoid hot baths, saunas, steam rooms, tight clothing, and intense workouts for at least 24 hours.
- **No Scrubbing Yet:** Wait at least 48 hours before you start exfoliating the waxed area.
- **Prevent Ingrown Hairs:** After the initial 48-hour period, start gently exfoliating the area 2 to 3 times a week and use a lightweight, non-comedogenic moisturizer to keep the skin soft and allow new hairs to push through easily.
