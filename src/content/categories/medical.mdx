## What to Expect After a Clinical Skin Treatment

Because clinical treatments work at a much deeper level than standard facials, your skin requires a specific recovery period. The intensity of your aftercare will depend on the procedure you had (e.g., Fractional Laser vs. Plasmage).

### The First 48 Hours
- **Expect Redness and Swelling:** It is completely normal for your skin to look red and feel hot, similar to a sunburn. Swelling is also common, particularly around delicate areas like the eyes after Plasmage.
- **Keep it Clean and Dry:** Follow your clinician's exact instructions regarding washing. In many cases, you must keep the area completely dry for the first 24 to 48 hours to allow the skin to begin the healing process safely.
- **No Active Skincare:** Absolutely no Retinol, Vitamin C, or exfoliating acids. Use only the specific healing ointments provided by your therapist.

### The Healing Process
- **Do Not Pick the Scabs:** Treatments like Plasmage create tiny crusts or scabs on the skin. You must let these fall off completely on their own (usually within 5 to 7 days). Picking them will lead to permanent scarring and pigmentation.
- **Strict Sun Avoidance:** Your newly resurfaced skin has zero defense against UV rays. You must stay out of direct sunlight and wear a wide-brimmed hat. Once your therapist advises it is safe to do so, you must apply SPF 50 daily without fail to prevent dark spots from forming on the healing tissue.
