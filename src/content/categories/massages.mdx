## How to Get the Most Out of Your Massage

To extend that post-massage feeling of relaxation and to help your muscles recover properly, follow these simple tips after your appointment.

### Hydrate, Hydrate, Hydrate
- **Drink Water Immediately:** Massage stimulates your lymphatic system and releases metabolic waste that has been trapped in tight muscles. You must drink plenty of water after your massage to help flush these toxins out of your body.
- **Skip the Caffeine and Alcohol:** For the rest of the day, try to avoid dehydrating drinks like coffee or alcohol, as they will counteract the benefits of the massage.

### Rest and Recover
- **Take it Easy:** Do not rush straight to the gym or into a highly stressful environment after a massage. Give your nervous system time to enjoy the relaxed state.
- **Expect Some Soreness:** If you had a Deep Tissue or Sports massage, it is completely normal to feel a bit sore or bruised the next day, similar to how you feel after a tough workout. This is a sign that the deeper muscle layers are healing.
- **Take a Warm Bath:** Soaking in a warm bath with Epsom salts later in the evening can help soothe any lingering soreness and further relax your muscles.
